Abstract 

Recent advancements in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have demon-strated impressive capabilities in interpreting single medical images. However, real-world clinical diagnosis is intrinsically a multi-view process, requiring the synthesis of informa-tion across volumetric slices, temporal se-quences, and comparative modalities. Ex-isting benchmarks fail to capture this com-plexity, limiting the assessment of models in realistic clinical workflows. To bridge this gap, we introduce MedMultiBench, the first large-scale benchmark specifically designed for medical multi-image understanding. Com-prising 11,392 expert-curated samples, Med-MultiBench evaluates MLLMs across four dis-tinct dimensions: Joint Reasoning, Compara-tive Analysis, Comprehensive Perception, and In-Context Learning. We benchmark 13 state-of-the-art MLLMs, revealing that while current models excel in single-view tasks, they struggle significantly with multi-image contexts. Our experiments identify a performance degrada-tion in open-source models when processing increased visual loads, whereas closed-source models demonstrate better scalability. Med-MultiBench provides a robust framework to facilitate the development of MLLMs capable of holistic clinical reasoning.

As illustrated in Figure 1, a radiologist rarely diagnoses a patient based on a single isolated slice. The diagnostic process requires scrolling through adjacent slices to trace anatomical structures, com-paring current scans with priors to track disease progression, or correlating findings across different imaging modalities. A practitioner relying on a single snapshot would be deemed clinically insuffi-cient (Tang et al., 2024). Consequently, relying on single-image evaluation creates a disparity between model performance metrics and real-world utility. Current medical VQA benchmarks, including VQA-RAD (Lau et al., 2018), SLAKE (Liu et al., 2021), and PMC-VQA (Zhang et al., 2023), remain predominantly restricted to single-image interpre-

While the general computer vision community has shifted toward multi-image (Zhao et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2025) and video-based understanding (Li et al., 2024), medical adapta-tions remain limited. Recent efforts like Med-FrameQA (Yu et al., 2025) introduce multi-frame inputs but are confined to instructional videos, fail-ing to capture broader diagnostic reasoning such as differential correspondence or spatial reconstruc-tion. Therefore, the lack of a systematic evaluation framework leaves the abilities of medical MLLMs in complex clinical reasoning unquantifiable. To bridge this gap, we introduce MedMulti-Bench , a comprehensive benchmark specifically engineered to evaluate the multi-image understand-ing capabilities of MLLMs. Moving beyond simple image-text pairing, MedMultiBench requires the holistic synthesis of temporal, spatial, and com-parative evidence across more than 11,000 expert-curated samples. We formalize medical multi-image reasoning into a novel taxonomy compris-ing four essential dimensions: (1) Joint Reason-ing for cross-view feature fusion; (2) Compara-tive Analysis for differential diagnosis; (3) Compre-hensive Perception for higher-order logic such as spatial ordering and temporal analysis; and (4) In-Context Learning for few-shot adaptability using interleaved clinical demonstrations. Our contribu-tions are as follows: (1) We establish a formal taxonomy for multi-image medical reasoning, defining inter-image rela-tionships through four key dimensions: spatiotem-poral continuity, cross-view heterogeneity, differ-ential correspondence, and semantic reference. (2) We introduce MedMultiBench, the first large-scale benchmark for multi-image clinical under-standing, comprising 11,392 samples across 13 subtasks to rigorously evaluate holistic evidence synthesis in realistic scenarios. (3) We conduct an extensive evaluation of 13 state-of-the-art MLLMs. Our experiments reveal that current open-source models suffer significantly from information overload in multi-image scenar-ios compared to closed-source counterparts, high-lighting critical directions for future optimization. 

2 Related Work 

2.1 Multimodal Large Language Models 

Recent multimodal large language models have achieved significant progress in vision tasks (Hurst et al., 2024; Team et al., 2023; Yang et al., 2025a). However, the performance of these models in med-ical domain is constrained due to the lack of spe-cialized medical knowledge. To bridge this gap, medical MLLMs such as LLaVA-Med (Li et al., 2023), HuatuoGPT-Vision (Chen et al., 2024), Med-Flamingo (Moor et al., 2023), Lingshu (Xu et al., 2025) and Fleming-VL (Shu et al., 2025) have been developed and achieve better scores on existing medical VQA benchmarks. 

2.2 Medical Vision-Language Benchmarks 

The landscape of medical VQA has evolved sig-nificantly. Early datasets like VQA-RAD (Lau et al., 2018) and PathVQA (He et al., 2020) es-tablished the baseline for closed-ended and open-ended QA on Radiology and Pathology images. Additionally, SLAKE (Liu et al., 2021) introduced bilingual annotations and richer semantic segmen-tation masks, while PMC-VQA (Zhang et al., 2023) scaled up the data magnitude by mining image-text pairs from open-access literature. More recently, works like OmniMedVQA (Hu et al., 2024) and MLEBench (Xu et al., 2024) expanded the scope by providing large-scale, comprehensive evaluation across more medical modalities and tasks. To move beyond simple perception, MedXpertQA (Zuo et al., 2025) introduced a benchmark specifically designed to test expert-level clinical reasoning, fo-cusing on challenging cases that require deep med-ical knowledge. Despite their contributions, these benchmarks share a common limitation: inputs are restricted to single image. This restriction prevents the assessment of cross-referencing skills, which are vital for reducing diagnostic ambiguity. 

2.3 Multi-Image Understanding Benchmark 

In the general domain, evaluating the capabil-ity of MLLMs to reason across multiple vi-sual inputs has become a pivotal research di-rection (Meng et al., 2024; Jiang et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024). In the medical domain, Med-MIM (Yang et al., 2025b) proposed a multi-image instruction-following benchmark to evaluate the multi-image understanding ability of MLLMs. MedFrameQA (Yu et al., 2025) extracts temporally coherent frames from videos to evaluate the reason-ing capabilities of MLLMs in real-world clinical diagnosis. However, existing benchmarks focus on limited modalities or simple tasks and cannot effec-tively simulate real-world clinical scenarios. 3 Preliminary 

While standard MLLMs optimize the conditional probability P (y | I, T ) for single image con-texts (Huang et al., 2022), this paradigm often re-duces medical diagnostics to static instance recog-nition. Such a simplified approach fails to account for the multi-source evidence synthesis essential to clinical decision-making. We therefore define 

multi-image medical reasoning as generating a re-sponse y given a textual instruction T and a set of 

N images I = {I1, . . . , I N } where N ≥ 2:

y = argmax

P (y | I , T ). (1) To succeed in this task, a model must learn an interaction function that captures the underlying relational structure of I. We characterize these relationships through four primary dimensions. 

Spatiotemporal Continuity. This category en-compasses images constrained by physical or tem-poral proximity, simulating the foundational pro-cess of navigating volumetric or longitudinal medi-cal data. In volumetric scenarios, the set I consists of sequential 2D slices that represent a continuous 3D anatomical structure. The model is required to understand the spatial arrangement of these slices to maintain structural coherence. Similarly, for tem-poral data, the images capture the same anatomical region at different time points. Reasoning in this context shifts from static recognition to analyzing transitions, such as tracking tumor progression or evaluating treatment response over time. 

Cross-view Heterogeneity. Images in this cat-egory represent the same subject through diverse imaging physics or viewpoints. This includes multi-modal correlation between MRI and CT scans or multi-angle projections such as frontal and lateral X-rays. Unlike continuity-based tasks, this dimen-sion requires synergistic integration; the model must perform cross-modal alignment to resolve ambiguities, effectively triangulating the diagnosis through diverse sensory inputs. 

Differential Correspondence. This category fo-cuses on image pairs curated for comparative anal-ysis to identify subtle pathological or physiological shifts. Typical clinical scenarios include evaluat-ing contrast enhancement between pre- and post-contrast phases. The model must distinguish persis-tent anatomical structures from interval changes. 

Semantic Reference. Unlike other categories, these images are linked by analogical relevance rather than patient identity. This dimension encom-passes visual retrieval based on shared attributes and in-context learning, where the image set I con-tains support demonstrations consisting of image-text pairs {Ik, T k}. In these cases, the model must perform analogical reasoning to map patterns from reference examples to the target query, necessitat-ing a meta-level understanding of diagnostic logic.

4.1 Task 1: Joint Reasoning 

This task evaluates the ability to synthesize seman-tic information from multiple visual inputs, primar-ily targeting the cross-view heterogeneity. 

Organ Recognition and Disease Detection. 

These subtasks assess fine-grained identification across different planes, such as axial and coronal views, or pathological diagnoses requiring feature fusion from multiple complementary modalities. We constructed 1019 organ recognition examples and 703 disease detection examples from GMAI. 

Segmentation Mask Analysis. This subtask aims to explore the ability to visually align the orig-inal image I with a binary mask M ∈ { 0, 1}H×W .Unlike standard VQA, the model must map the anatomical structures represented by M onto I

to identify highlighted regions of interest without pixel-level preprocessing. We screened 978 sam-ples from SAM-Med2D, containing 25 classes of anatomical structures. 

Multi-image Description. Real-world clinical scenarios require models to generate clinical re-ports based on multi-view inputs, such as frontal and lateral X-rays, or CT modalities and X-ray modalities. This necessitates the model’s ability to synthesize multi-view evidence into a structured narrative. We filtered 992 samples from IU-Xray and MedPix to obtain a standard, high-quality re-port generation test set with multi-image inputs. 

4.2 Task 2: Comparative Analysis 

Clinical diagnosis is inherently differential. This category maps to the differential correspondence by evaluating the discriminative power between related visual instances. 

Visual Choice and Visual Retrieval. Visual choice employs a reverse VQA paradigm, requir-ing the model to select the correct image ( Ik) from a candidate image set O = {I1, I 2, . . . , I M } that best matches the clinical description q. The core objective is to evaluate how the model can differen-tiate between multiple visual candidates and make 27379 decisions based on professional medical cues. Vi-sual Retrieval focuses on semantic attribute consis-tency retrieval across images. Specifically, given a query image Iquery and a text description q for a specific semantic attribute, the model must iden-tify the target image from the candidate image set 

O that is consistent with Iquery in the dimension specified by q. We construct 989 visual choice examples and 835 visual retrieval examples from OmniMedVQA, each example including 4 image options. 

Difference Description. Given a pair of im-ages IA and IB , the model must generate a struc-tured description ∆ to describe clinically signifi-cant changes. This task requires the model to accu-rately compare two medical images with different diagnoses or presentations, identify key differences, and output a difference report in professional and clear text. We select 1022 discrepancy samples from IU-Xray and MedPix, and generate reports using LLM through a carefully constructed prompt. The reports are then verified by experts. 

4.3 Task 3: Comprehensive Perceptions 

This category focuses on the advanced cognitive and comprehensive reasoning capabilities neces-sary for models in complex medical scenarios in-volving multiple image sequences. It goes be-yond static description and comparison of single or paired images, focusing on analysis over time, ranking at spatial scales, and precise statistics in quantitative dimensions. 

Temporal Analysis. Given a chronologically ordered sequence of medical images, such as mul-tiple CT scans of a patient, the model is required to analyze the dynamic changes in lesions or physi-ological structures. We selected 702 high-quality samples from Med-MIM as our test set.

4.4 Task 4: In-Context Learning 

Finally, we evaluate the few-shot adaptability of models through the lens of semantic reference. Us-ing interleaved image-text prompts, the model is provided with a support set of k demonstrations to infer task specifications without parameter updates, as represented by the conditional probability: 

P (y | (I1, T 1), . . . , (Ik, T k), I query , T query ). (2) This task measures the capacity for analogical rea-soning and the ability to map complex diagnostic logic from limited reference examples to novel tar-get queries. To ensure a rigorous evaluation, we construct 1,053 instances for challenging closed-ended VQA tasks such as counting and spatial sort-ing, alongside 992 instances for open-ended VQA tasks involving multi-image descriptions. Each task is evaluated under 1-shot, 3-shot, and 5-shot set-tings to observe the scaling of model performance with increasing context. 

4.5 Dataset Statistics and Human Check 

MedMultiBench contains 11,392 carefully selected samples covering a variety of medical imaging modalities and diagnostic challenges. The dataset distribution is strategically balanced across four core capabilities to ensure robustness of the evalua-tion. To guarantee clinical validity, all samples un-derwent a rigorous human verification by medical professionals to ensure accurate alignment between visual evidence and textual descriptions. 5.3 Modality Sensitivity Analysis 

The robustness of evaluated models across 10 dif-ferent medical imaging modalities is illustrated in Figure 4. Overall, the results reveal significant per-formance variance depending on the imaging tech-nique, with closed-source models generally main-taining more balanced performance profiles across the modality spectrum. Gemini3-Pro exhibits the most comprehensive coverage, consistently achieving top-tier accuracy across nearly all modalities, particularly in CT, MRI, and Endoscopy. This suggests a superior gen-eralized capability in handling cross-sectional radi-ological data. Other closed-source models, such as GPT-5.1 and Doubao-Seed-1.6, also show strong performance, though their advantages are more modality-specific. In the open-source category, models dis-play more pronounced strengths and weaknesses. HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B shows exceptional special-ized performance in OCT, where it rivals or even surpasses closed-source counterparts. However, its performance fluctuates in more traditional ra-diological modalities like CT and MRI. Similarly, Fleming-VL-8B and Qwen3-VL-8B exhibit com-petitive results in X-ray and Histopathology, yet show a noticeable performance drop in complex microscopic or dermoscopic images.

6 Conclusion 

In this work, we address the limitations of single-view medical evaluation by introducing MedMulti-Bench, a comprehensive benchmark dedicated to multi-image clinical reasoning. We established a novel taxonomy that categorizes multi-image rela-tionships into Joint Reasoning, Comparative Anal-ysis, Comprehensive Perception, and In-Context Learning. By curating 11,392 expert-verified sam-ples across 13 subtasks, we provide a rigorous 27383 testbed for assessing MLLMs in realistic diagnos-tic scenarios. Our extensive evaluation exposes a significant disparity between open-source and closed-source models. We hope MedMultiBench will serve as a foundational resource to guide the development of next-generation medical MLLMs capable of holistic clinical understanding. 

Limitations 

While MedMultiBench advances the evaluation of multi-image understanding, several limitations re-main. First, although we simulate volumetric anal-ysis using sequential 2D slices, this approach does not fully capture the native 3D processing capabil-ities required for raw volumetric data (e.g., NIfTI files). Second, for open-ended generation tasks, while we employ widely used metrics like Rouge-L and Bert Score, these automated metrics may not perfectly align with human expert judgment regard-ing clinical nuance and factual correctness. Future iterations will aim to incorporate native 3D data for-mats and more granular, clinically grounded evalu-ation metrics. 

Ethical Considerations 

The images and original textual data utilized in MedMultiBench are derived exclusively from pub-licly available datasets (e.g., IU-Xray, MedPix, GMAI) and are used in strict compliance with their respective licenses. No private or identifiable pa-tient data was collected for this study. To ensure data quality and diversity, portions of the textual de-scriptions and questions were reformatted or rewrit-ten using GPT-5 based on the original clinical anno-tations. We strictly controlled this process to func-tion as a stylistic refinement rather than content generation. All AI-processed text underwent rigor-ous manual verification by medical professionals to ensure clinical accuracy and to eliminate potential hallucinations.

A.1 Details on Data Source 

To construct our benchmark, we curated data from the following high-quality sources, covering a wide spectrum of modalities, specialties, and tasks: 

GMAI-MMBench (Ye et al., 2024): This datasets collects data from public sources and hos-pitals, covering medical imaging tasks of detection, classification, and segmentation. It is constructed from 284 datasets across 38 medical image modali-ties, 18 clinical-related tasks, 18 departments, and 4 perceptual granularities in a Visual Question An-swering (VQA) format. 

SAM-Med2D (Cheng et al., 2023): SAM-Med2D contains 4.6 million medical images and 19.7 million corresponding masks, covering 10 modalities, 31 major organs, and 219 categories. Its segmentation targets cover almost the entire body, making it a large-scale and diverse medical image segmentation dataset. 

IU-Xray (Demner-Fushman et al., 2015): Sourced from the Indiana University Healthcare system, this dataset contains 7,470 chest X-ray im-ages associated with 3,955 clinical reports. Each clinical case typically includes both frontal and lateral views of the same patient. This dual-view characteristic is particularly valuable for our bench-mark, as it provides the foundation for testing a model’s ability to perform volumetric integration and spatial reasoning across different perspectives. 

MedPix (Siragusa et al., 2025): An expert-curated database featuring over 12,000 patient cases, 9000 topics and nearly 59,000 images. Each case is categorized by disease location, patient pro-file, and pathology. The dataset is characterized by high-quality, peer-reviewed clinical cases. We leverage this resource for its high-fidelity expert knowledge and the complex, multi-modal relation-ships between patient history and visual evidence. 

OmniMedVQA (Hu et al., 2024): OmniMed-VQA is a large-scale, comprehensive VQA bench-mark containing 118,010 images with 127,995 QA-items, covering 12 different modalities and refer-ring to more than 20 human anatomical regions, including rare anatomical regions. Its scale and diversity make it an ideal source for constructing questions that require broad medical knowledge and the ability to generalize across infrequently seen medical conditions. 

MMMU (Yue et al., 2024): MMMU (Massive Multi-discipline Multimodal Understanding and Reasoning Benchmark) is a benchmark designed to evaluate the performance of multimodal models on large-scale, multidisciplinary tasks. We select our data from its subset Health and Medicine, which requires deep domain-specific knowledge and com-plex reasoning, often involving the interpretation of charts, molecular structures, and intricate clinical diagrams. 

Med-MIM (Yang et al., 2025b): Med-MIM is a specialized dataset focused on multi-image medi-cal understanding, providing an instruction-tuning 27386 dataset and evaluation suite. It includes 83.2k multi-image medical instruction samples across four tasks: co-reference, comparison, reasoning, and temporal understanding. 

MedFrameQA (Yu et al., 2025): Derived from medical videos (such as laparoscopy and en-doscopy), MedFrameQA dataset comprises 2,851 VQA pairs (gathered from 9,237 high-quality frames in 3,420 videos), covering nine human body systems and 43 organs; every question is accompa-nied by two to five images. It focuses on surgical workflows and dynamic anatomical changes. This dataset is unique for its emphasis on sequential reasoning, challenging models to understand how clinical events unfold over time across a series of visual inputs rather than in a single static image. 

A.2 Details on Baselines 

To ensure a comprehensive evaluation of Med-MultiBench, we conduct experiments on a di-verse set of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), ranging from specialized medical mod-els to general-domain frontier models. 

Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct (Bai et al., 2025): As a base model widely used for fine-tuning in spe-cialized fields, this model features a 7B parameter architecture optimized for high-resolution visual perception and structured output. Pre-trained on a massive corpus of interleaved image-text data, it excels at recognizing fine-grained visual de-tails. We include it to evaluate how standard, high-performance open-source MLLMs handle special-ized medical multi-image contexts. 

Qwen3-VL-8B-Instruct (Yang et al., 2025a): This model represents the latest generation of the Qwen-VL family, specifically enhanced for long-horizon temporal reasoning and video-level un-derstanding. With an upgraded vision-language adapter and expanded context window, it is de-signed to maintain coherence across multiple vi-sual inputs. It serves as a key baseline for test-ing whether recent advancements in general multi-image processing translate to clinical scenarios. 

Lingshu-7B (Xu et al., 2025): Built upon the Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct architecture, Lingshu is a specialized medical MLLM designed to overcome the limitations of general-domain models in clin-ical scenarios. It utilizes a comprehensive data curation procedure that synthesizes accurate med-ical captions and reasoning samples from diverse sources, including imaging and extensive medi-cal texts. The training recipe follows a “shallow-to-deep” progression and comprises four sequen-tial stages: (1) MedicalShallowAlignment on 927k data, (2) MedicalDeepAlignment on 4.1M data, (3) MedicalInstructionTuning on 7.1M data, and (4) Medical-oriented Reinforcement Learning on 100K data. We include Lingshu to evaluate the impact of specialized medical alignment and reasoning-enhanced training on multi-image clinical tasks. 

Fleming-VL-8B (Shu et al., 2025): Fleming-VL-8B is a unified multimodal framework built upon the InternVL3 architecture (InternViT as vision encoder + Qwen2.5 as Language Model), specifi-cally designed for heterogeneous medical modali-ties including 2D images, 3D scans, and temporal videos. The model is trained with a three-stage training paradigm: (1) Interleaved Pre-training on 2M visual-language corpora to develop multi-image reasoning; (2) Medical Knowledge Injection via 4M image-caption pairs for cross-modal align-ment; and (3) Instruction Tuning on 2M instruction-response pairs and GRPO-based Reinforcement Learning on 100K samples to refine systematic clinical reasoning. We include Fleming-VL-8B to assess how structural optimizations for long-context visual sequences impact performance in complex multi-image medical benchmarks. 

HuatuoGPT-Vision-7B / 34B (Chen et al., 2024): The HuatuoGPT-Vision series (7B and 34B) is trained on 1.3 million high-quality medical image-text pairs. The training follows a two-stage paradigm: (1) large-scale medical image-text align-ment, aimed at injecting fundamental medical vi-sual knowledge; and (2) medical visual instruction fine-tuning, utilizing expert-annotated and synthe-sized question-answer pairs to enhance clinical di-agnostic reasoning capabilities. We introduce two versions, 7B and 34B, based on Qwen2-VL and Yi-1.5-34B respectively, to evaluate the impact of parameter scale and large-scale medical knowledge injection on complex multi-image clinical tasks. 

Moonshot-v1 (MoonshotAI, 2025): Moonshot-v1 is a prominent proprietary MLLM recognized for its exceptional long-context processing capa-bilities. It excels in maintaining semantic global coherence across dense, interleaved image-text se-quences, making it a robust baseline for evaluating long-form medical information understanding. 27387 Doubao-Seed-v1.6 (Bytedance, 2025): Devel-oped by ByteDance, Doubao-Seed-v1.6 is a highly capable model that prioritizes logical reasoning and precise instruction-following. It’s specifically designed to navigate complex, multi-step reason-ing trajectories, providing a unique perspective on deliberate clinical deduction. 

Qwen-VL-Plus (Bai et al., 2023): Qwen-VL-Plus is a versatile commercial-grade model from Alibaba Cloud that demonstrates a balanced profi-ciency in reasoning depth and broad cross-domain knowledge. It serves as a reliable and stable base-line for general-purpose multimodal understanding across various clinical scenarios. 

GLM-4.5v (Hong et al., 2025): GLM-4.5v is a leading Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) multimodal model developed by Zhipu AI. It is distinguished by its sophisticated vision-language fusion and is particularly refined for complex scientific interpre-tation, structured data analysis, and long-document integration. 

GPT-4o / GPT-5.1 (Hurst et al., 2024): These represent the frontier of native multimodal mod-els from OpenAI, setting the gold standard for seamless text-vision integration. While GPT-4o provides world-class perceptual accuracy, GPT-5.1 introduces advanced agentic reasoning, capable of high-level cognitive synthesis in expert-level multi-modal tasks. 

Gemini3-Pro (Team et al., 2023): Gemini3-Pro is a high-performance, large-scale multimodal model from Google DeepMind’s Gemini series, designed for complex reasoning and professional-grade applications. This model supports the unified understanding and generation of information across multiple modalities, including text and images, and has been optimized for tasks such as long-context modeling, code generation, and logical reasoning, making it suitable for research and industrial use.
