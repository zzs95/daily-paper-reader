## Abstract:

Ultrasound imaging is a cornerstone of disease screening, yet its clinical utility is often limited by time-intensive and operator-dependent interpretation, as well as variability in qualitative assessment. In particular, extracting consistent quantitative parameters from multi-view and multi-modal ultrasound sequences (e.g., echocardiography, vascular ultrasound) remains laborious, limiting diagnostic throughput and reproducibility. While deep learning offers automation potential, most existing approaches either ignore clinically established measurement assistant workflows or treat regression and classification as dis joint tasks, sacrificing interpretability and physiological grounding. To address this, we propose EchoReason, an end-to-end two stage vision-language framework that follows a measurement assisted diagnosis paradigm. EchoReason first estimates inter pretable cardiovascular parameters from heterogeneous ultra sound inputs using modality-aware expert–adaptor modules, and then performs disease diagnosis by integrating visual evidence with these measurements and explicit parameter–disease association guidance within a unified vision–language decoder. This design yields accurate predictions while providing auditable in termediate measurements that support evidence-based interpretation. Evaluated on multi-modal, multi-view ultrasound cohort, the model achieves state-of-the-art performance in both parameter estimation and multi-disease classification, demonstrating that clinically structured architectures can improve accuracy, robustness, and interpretability for ultrasound-based decision support.
