## Abstract:

The deployment of sophisticated AI segmentation models faces major obstacles when used in clinical settings with limited resources because these systems need powerful computers and complex interpretation methods, which prevent regional hospitals and remote healthcare facilities from using precision diagnostic tools. The research introduces AGENT-DIFF-X as a consumer-adaptive system that unites agentic intelligence with diffusion models and uncertainty-based explainability to achieve exact 3D breast tumor segmentation on high-performance servers and consumer-grade hardware. The Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) controller in AGENT-DIFF-X operates as a dynamic system that adjusts diffusion sampling parameters through three mechanisms that depend on both case complexity and available system resources to achieve smooth performance in all deployment environments. The system includes an agent-based uncertainty quantification module, which generates Bayesian confidence maps to assist controllers and delivers clinicians diagnostic tools that display reliable indicators. The experimental results from MRI datasets show that AGENT-DIFF-X outperforms all existing baselines (UNet, TransUNet, SwinUNet, Diff-UNet) through its high segmentation accuracy (92.1% Dice, 4.42mm Hausdorff Distance) and its excellent uncertainty calibration (Expected Calibration Error (ECE): 0.037 vs. 0.059). The framework enables operational medical care delivery through AI-based precise medicine because its flexible design enables both telemedicine operations and treatment of patients in edge devices.
