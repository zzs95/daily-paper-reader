# TopoSFI-MUNet: a topological spatial–frequency 

# interaction network for cross-modal medical image 

# segmentation 

Journal of King Saud University Computer and Information Sciences https://doi.org/10.1007/s44443-026-00977-z Received: 27 Mar 2026 Accepted: 17 Jun 2026 Tao Zhou, Qitao Liu, Huiling Lu, Ke Song, Shiyi Yu & Kaixiong Chen ARTICLE IN PRESS TopoSFI-MUNet: A Topological Spatial –Frequency Interaction Network for Cross-Modal Medical Image Segmentation 

Abstract 

Since different imaging modalities provide complementary tissue structure and lesion information, multi-modal medical image semantic segmentation can significantly improve the ability to identify and locate lesion regions by fusing multi-source image features. However, the existing methods usually model the segmentation task as a pixel-level independent classification problem, ignoring the topological relationship among pixels, and it is difficult to effectively fuse the cross-modal spatial and frequency domain features from the perspective of topological relationship, resulting in blurred lesion boundaries and incomplete lesion contour structure expression. To solve the above problems, this paper proposes a topological spatial –frequency interaction network for cross-modal medical image segmentation. By explicitly modeling the connectivity relationship among pixels and combining the spatial domain and frequency domain information, the cross-modal connectivity topological features are enhanced. Specifically, this paper constructs a three-encoder single-decoder multi-modal segmentation framework based on connected graph modeling, and introduces three types of masks such as connected region, lesion contour and lesion edge for joint supervision to enhance the ability of the model to perceive the edge of the lesion and the contour of the lesion. In the network structure, a cross-modal "frequency-channel, spatial-location" topology feature compensation module is designed in the skip connection stage to enhance the topology information transmission between the encoder and the decoder. In the bottleneck layer, a cross-modal "spatial-channel, frequency-location" topological feature enhancement module is proposed, which decomposes the cross-modal connectivity topological features at high and low frequencies through discrete wavelet transform, and combines the self-attention mechanism to realize the interactive fusion of spatial domain and frequency domain information, so as to improve the expression ability of deep cross-modal topological features. Compared with the baseline model, on the lung tumor dataset, mIoU, Dice, VOE, RVD and Recall are increased by 3.03%, 2.34%, 4.76%, 3.68% and 2.3%, respectively. On the brain tumor dataset, mIoU, Dice, VOE, RVD and Recall are increased by 3.69%,2.93%, 6.23%, 4.37% and 4.06%,respectively, which proves the effectiveness of the method. 

Keywords: Medical Image segmentation, U-Net, Connected Graph, Connectivity Topological Features, Spatial-Frequency Feature Fusion 

1. Introduction 

Medical image semantic segmentation is a key technology in the field of medical image analysis. Its task is to identify and label the target structure (such as tumor, organ or lesion area) in the image at the pixel level, which is of great significance for assisting clinical diagnosis and treatment decision-making[1]. Compared with traditional boundary detection or region recognition methods, semantic segmentation can more accurately describe the location and shape of the lesion area through the pixel-level prediction results, thus providing a reliable quantitative basis for lesion size measurement, morphological analysis and disease progression assessment. In recent years, rapid advances in deep learning, particularly the widespread adoption of CNNs and vision Transformers in medical image analysis, have significantly promoted the development of medical image semantic segmentation[2]. Deep learning models can automatically learn multi-level feature representations, which can significantly improve the generalization ability and prediction accuracy of segmentation models. On this basis, by fusing the complementary information between different imaging modalities, the multi-modal medical image semantic segmentation method can not only improve the accuracy of lesion area localization and boundary recognition, but also alleviate the problems of noise interference and insufficient structural information in ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS single-modal images to a certain extent, so as to provide more comprehensive and reliable results for clinical diagnosis. At present, the core of multi-modal medical image segmentation methods is to use the complementary characteristics between different modality images to make up for the limitations of single modality in feature expression[3]. For example, Zhu et al.[4] proposed a cross-modal spatial information enhancement method, which effectively alleviates the problems of insufficient multi-modal feature expression and fuzzy lesion boundaries by strengthening the spatial feature interaction between different modalities. Zhou et al. [5]proposed Guide-YNet, an interactive segmentation architecture that exploits lesion position cues from PET images to guide the network in learning discriminative features. By incorporating modality-dependent representations, the model improves its ability to detect lesion regions. Li et al.[6] introduced the Semi-MedSAM framework, in which a MoGAE-based encoder is designed to accommodate heterogeneous multi-modal information and dynamically capture various lesion contexts. Yin et al. [7]proposed a Transformer-based multi-modal self-supervised learning method, which realized the effective segmentation of retinal vascular structures by modeling the complementary features between multi-modalities and combining the filtering mechanism. In general, the existing multi-modal medical image segmentation research has gradually developed from simple feature fusion to cross-modal semantic correlation modeling, deep feature interaction and global structure perception, and has made significant progress in the recognition task of complex lesion regions. However, most existing methods still model the medical image segmentation task as a pixel-level independent classification problem, ignoring the inherent topological information among pixels, and lack a mechanism for collaborative fusion of cross-modal spatial domain and frequency domain features from the perspective of topological relationship. When the network deals with complex anatomical structures or lesion boundary regions, this kind of modeling method is difficult to accurately depict the edge and contour of the lesion region, which is easy to cause boundary ambiguity and incomplete lesion contour expression, especially in the case of complex lesion edge structure or low contrast between foreground and background. From the above comparison, it can be seen that the traditional U-Net method has certain limitations in characterizing the lesion boundary and lesion contour, which is prone to the problem of discontinuous lesion boundary or incomplete contour structure. The segmentation method based on connectivity modeling provides more detailed spatial structure constraints for the network by introducing the topological relationship among pixels and their neighborhood pixels. For example, in the connected label shown in the bottom left corner of Figure 1, each pixel establishes a connected relationship with its eight neighboring pixels: when all neighboring pixels are background, the pixel is regarded as a background pixel; When all neighboring pixels belong to the foreground region, the pixel is judged as an internal foreground pixel. When there are both foreground and background pixels in the neighborhood, the pixel is at the edge of the lesion. By explicitly modeling the connectivity relationship among pixels, the connectivity segmentation method can more accurately depict the continuity of the lesion region boundary and the integrity of the lesion contour, thereby improving the accuracy of the segmentation results. Based on the above analysis, this paper proposes a cross-modal spatial-frequency interactive semantic segmentation network based on topological feature enhancement for multi-modal medical image segmentation. From the perspective of topological relationship among pixels, the spatial domain and frequency domain information are jointly modeled, and the effective fusion of cross-modal features is realized, so as to improve the perception ability of complex lesion region boundaries and contours. Specifically, this paper constructs a three-encoder single-decoder multi-modal segmentation framework based on connected graph modeling, and jointly supervised by three masks of connected region, lesion region and lesion edge, so that the model can simultaneously learn regional semantic information, topological structure information and edge structure features, thereby enhancing the perception ability of the lesion boundary structure. The main contributions of this paper are as follows: (1) Cross-modal spatial-frequency interactive segmentation framework based on topological feature enhancement: Existing methods ignore the topological information among pixels and it is difficult to fuse cross-modal spatial-frequency features from the perspective of topological relationship. Therefore, a cross-modal space-frequency interactive semantic segmentation network based on topological feature enhancement is proposed. By explicitly modeling the connectivity topological relationship among pixels and jointly using the spatial and frequency domain information, the framework enhances the cross-modal topological features, so as to effectively improve the perception ability of the edges and contours of complex lesions. (2) Cross-modal topology feature compensation module: a cross-modal frequency-channel, spatial-location topology feature compensation module is designed in the skip connection stage of the network. The module extracts and fuses the cross-modal connectivity topological features through connectivity transformation, and combines the attention mechanism for fine-grained enhancement, so that the topological structure information in ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS the encoder can be effectively transmitted to the decoder, so as to make up for the topological information difference between the encoder and the decoder, and the topological consistency between deep and shallow features can be enhanced. (3) Cross-modal topological feature enhancement module: a cross-modal "spatial-channel, frequency-location" topological feature enhancement module is proposed in the network bottleneck layer. The module uses Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) to decompose the cross-modal connectivity topological features at high and low frequencies, and combines frequency-position attention and spatial-channel attention to realize the interactive fusion between spatial domain and frequency domain features, so as to significantly enhance the expression ability of deep cross-modal topological features. In summary, by modeling the topological relationship among pixels, constructing cross-modal connectivity topological feature representation, and jointly optimizing the spatial domain and frequency domain information, the proposed method effectively enhances the perception and expression ability of the model for the edge and contour of the lesion, thereby significantly improving the segmentation performance of the lesion region in multi-modal medical images. 

2. Related work 

2.1 Multi-modal Medical image segmentation 

Multi-modal medical image segmentation aims to comprehensively utilize the complementary information between different imaging modalities to obtain more accurate and stable lesion area recognition results. Different modalities can usually reflect the lesion characteristics from multiple perspectives such as structure, function or tissue contrast. Therefore, effective integration of multi-modal information has become an important research direction to improve the performance of medical image segmentation. In recent years, a large number of studies have focused on cross-modal feature fusion mechanisms. Chen et al. [8]proposed a semantic segmentation network based on multi-modal alignment fusion. By introducing a dynamic visual state space module and a cross-attention alignment fusion strategy, the features between different modalities can effectively interact in a unified representation space, thereby improving the stability and expression ability of multi-modal fusion. Jing et al.[9] developed HyCon, a two-stage semi-supervised learning strategy that applies knowledge distillation to align cross-modal samples with similar topological characteristics, enabling more effective multi-modal segmentation. Guo et al. [10]incorporated TCUnet as the generator in a GAN-based framework to perform target image segmentation, while a critic network is further constructed to jointly model latent representations and hierarchical modality-specific features. Zhou et al. [11]designed a multi-modal feature decoupling fusion network, which modeled location, edge and content features separately and combined with a joint semantic-driven strategy to realize the fine segmentation of lung tumor regions. In general, the existing multi-modal segmentation methods have made significant progress in cross-modal feature interaction and fusion. However, most researches still focus on the alignment and fusion mechanism of spatial domain features, and pay insufficient attention to the expression of structural information of different modalities in the frequency domain and spatial-frequency collaborative modeling, thus there are still some limitations between global structural consistency and local detail description. 

2.2 Frequency domain segmentation method 

In order to further enhance the expression ability of the model for image structure information, some studies begin to analyze and model image features from the perspective of frequency domain. Compared with the segmentation methods that only rely on spatial domain information, the frequency domain method can describe the global structure and local details of the image by decomposing different frequency components, thus showing certain advantages in boundary expression and structure consistency. Zhou et al. [12]proposed a frequency domain fusion framework with dual attention. By modeling low-frequency structural information and high-frequency boundary details respectively, and using global and local ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS attention mechanisms for adaptive fusion, the balance between overall structural consistency and local boundary accuracy is achieved. Hua et al. [13]constructed a semantic segmentation network based on multi-frequency wavelet decomposition, which realized effective expression of multi-scale semantic information through parallel decomposition of high-frequency and low-frequency components and multi-directional filtering mechanism. Zhou et al.[14] introduced a shape-aware partition framework with a dual-branch architecture, where the high-frequency branch captures edge details and the low-frequency branch focuses on global shape information. These complementary features are fused through a hybrid decoder and a chimeric wavelet block to enable effective bidirectional information interaction. Yan et al. [15]proposed a multi-scale attention-driven frequency-domain enhanced Transformer framework, which uses Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT) to extract multi-scale frequency features and combines inverse attention mechanism to model the dependence between different scales, so as to alleviate the semantic differences between encoder and decoder. In addition, some studies have further explored the cooperative modeling mechanism between the spatial and frequency domains. Zhou et al. [16]developed a bidirectional cross-modal segmentation framework that integrates spatial-domain and frequency-domain 

representations, thereby improving the model’s ability to capture complicated lesion structures. Liu et al. [17]proposed a frequency-domain enhanced heterogeneous network, which adaptively extracts high-frequency components to strengthen the expression of boundary features, and fuses them with spatial domain features to improve the overall feature consistency. Although the above methods have achieved good results in enhancing the expression of boundary details and improving the global contour consistency, most of the existing research focuses on frequency feature enhancement and spatial-frequency feature fusion mechanism, lacking explicit modeling of the connectivity and topological information among pixels in the image. Therefore, there are still some shortcomings in expressing the boundary continuity and contour integrity of complex lesion regions. 

2.3 Connected domain segmentation method 

To further improve the boundary continuity and contour integrity of the segmentation results, some studies begin to model the medical image segmentation task from the perspective of pixel connectivity and topological structure constraints. The connected domain segmentation method enables the network to more accurately perceive the edge and contour integrity of the target region by explicitly characterizing the connectivity among pixels and their neighborhood pixels. Thereby alleviating the common problems of boundary breakage or region fragmentation in traditional pixel classification methods. Yang et al. [18]proposed bilateral connectivity network, which effectively enhances the expression ability of the model for boundary structure by introducing an eight-channel connectivity mask and a single-channel binary mask for joint supervision. Subsequently, Yang et al. [19]proposed a directional connectivity modeling strategy, which improved the sensitivity of the network to the structural changes of the lesion area by decoupling and strengthening the connectivity relationship features in different directions. Teng et al. [20]proposed the SP-DCNet framework, which uses pixel connectivity direction information for feature calibration and combines with global context modeling to achieve structure-sensitive medical image segmentation. Yang et al. [21]redefined the segmentation task as a joint optimization problem of pixel connectivity modeling and pixel-level tissue classification in CE-Net, which significantly improved the accuracy of esophageal disease detection. Deng et al. [22]further proposed a multi-task supervised Connected Region Attention Network (MTSL-RA) to enhance the discriminative ability of the model to tumor regional features through the regional attention mechanism. In general, the connected domain segmentation method shows clear advantages in alleviating boundary blurring and improving lesion contour integrity. However, the existing researches mainly focus on the connectivity structure modeling or the introduction of topological constraints, and pay insufficient attention to the cross-modal information fusion and the collaborative modeling mechanism of spatial domain and frequency domain, which makes it impossible to integrate the connectivity topological relationship among pixels to complete the ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS cross-modal spatial-frequency feature modeling.

3.1 Network architecture 

This paper proposes a cross-modal spatial-frequency interactive semantic segmentation network based on connectivity topology modeling (as shown in Figure 2). The overall network adopts a three-encoder single-decoder structure, which is used for fine segmentation of the lesion area in multi-modal medical images. Different from the traditional U-Net that relies on pixel-level classification, the eight-neighborhood connected graph modeling mechanism explicitly represents the connectivity topological relationship among pixels, which enhances the ability of the model to express the integrity of contours and the continuity of boundary details. Firstly, multi-modal medical images (such as CT, PET/CT and PET) are input into three independent encoders to extract feature representations of different modalities. In the encoding stage, the convolution and pooling operations are used to perform feature down-sampling layer-by-layer to obtain a multi-scale feature representation from low-level detail information to high-level semantic information. The features obtained in each layer of encoding stage are denoted as Ei(i=1,2,3,4). In the skip connection stage, a cross-modal "frequency-channel, spatial-location" topology feature compensation module is designed. The module captures connectivity topological features through connectivity transformation, uses Discrete Cosine Transform (DCT) to construct frequency-channel attention to capture multi-frequency structural information, and uses spatial-position attention to enhance the responsiveness of key regions, so as to realize fine-grained compensation of cross-modal connectivity topological features. The feature representation enhanced by this module is denoted as Ei' (i=1,2,3,4). In the network bottleneck layer, a cross-modal "spatial-channel, frequency-location" topology feature enhancement module is further introduced. On the one hand, the spatial-channel attention is used to capture the global context of cross-modal connectivity topological features in the channel dimension. On the other hand, the feature is decomposed into high frequency and low frequency components by Discrete Wavelet Transform (DWT), and the frequency-position attention mechanism is used to realize the interactive fusion between spatial and frequency information. Among them, the high-frequency component mainly focuses on edges and abnormal regions, while the low-frequency component focuses more on lesion contour continuity, and the deep feature representation output by this module is denoted as E5. In the decoding stage, the spatial resolution is gradually restored by layer-by-layer up-sampling and feature fusion, and the fine-grained connectivity topological features from skip ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS connections are combined for semantic reconstruction. The outputs of the decoder layers are denoted as Di (i=1,2,3,4). Finally, the network simultaneously output three types of prediction results: connected region prediction map, lesion contour prediction map and lesion edge prediction map. Through joint modeling and interactive fusion of the connectivity topological features in the spatial domain and frequency domain, the network can significantly enhance the perception ability of the model to the edge details and contour structure of the lesion, thereby improving the accuracy and stability of multi-modal medical image segmentation. 

3.2 Connected transformation and inverse connected transformation 

In the traditional U-Net framework, the network usually directly outputs single-channel semantic probability maps. Different from this, in this paper, the network output is redefined as an eight-channel connected topological map, and the training labels are converted to connected labels accordingly. To achieve this goal, two mapping processes, connected transformation and inverse connected transformation, are defined to convert between binary labels and connected labels.

4.1 Dataset and Experimental details 

To comprehensively evaluate the generalization capability and stability of the proposed model in multi-modal medical image segmentation, comparative and ablation experiments are conducted on the clinical lung tumor dataset and the publicly available BraTS2019 dataset. All experiments are performed under identical hardware and software environments to ensure result reproducibility and fair comparisons among different algorithms. 

4.1.1 Clinical lung tumor multi-modal dataset 

A clinical multi-modal lung tumor dataset is constructed based on imaging data acquired from a hospital in Yinchuan City, consisting of records from 200 patients with lung tumors. Three imaging modalities (PET, CT and PET/CT) are available for each patient, with each single-modal case containing 500 2D slices. Lesions are annotated at the pixel level by clinicians via slice-by-slice manual delineation with reference to multi-modal image features. The original resolution of all slices is 356×356. The preprocessing pipeline is described as follows. Firstly, slices without lesion regions and invalid slices severely affected by imaging artifacts were excluded. Next, random rotation and gray-level perturbation were adopted for data augmentation to expand sample diversity. All images were subsequently resampled to a unified resolution of 256×256 pixels. After preprocessing, 1800 valid 2D slices were obtained for each modality. Patient-level five-fold cross validation was adopted to partition the dataset. Two hundred patients were randomly assigned to five disjoint subsets, and all slices from each patient were retained in a single subset. When performing five-fold cross-validation, one subset was selected in turn as the independent test set for the current fold. It was only utilized for performance evaluation after training concluded and took no part in parameter updating or model selection throughout the entire training procedure. The remaining four patient subsets were 

merged to construct the candidate training pool for this fold (1440 slices in total). Without splitting any patient’s 

slices across groups, we randomly selected full patients whose cumulative slices amounted to 144 as the inner independent validation set, which was employed to select and store the optimal model weights during training. The leftover 1296 slices in the candidate training pool, excluding the validation set, served as the training set for gradient backpropagation and iterative parameter optimization. Ultimately, the mean and standard deviation calculated from the five folds of experimental results were taken as the final evaluation metrics. 

4.1.2 BraTS2019 public data set 

Public multi-modal datasets for lung tumor segmentation are limited. To further verify the generalization performance of the model, the BraTS2019 brain tumor dataset is adopted for additional experiments. Three MRI modalities, including FLAIR, T1 and T2, are selected. To meet the input requirements of 2D networks, all 3D volumes are extracted into 2D slices and saved in PNG format. All images are uniformly resized to a resolution of 256×256. A total of 1745 multi-modal samples are obtained after preprocessing. This dataset is also evaluated via a case-level five-fold cross-validation scheme, and its partitioning rules are consistent with those of the aforementioned clinical multi-modal lung tumor dataset. 

4.1.3 Experimental environment and training parameters 

All models are implemented on the PyTorch framework. Experiments are run on a server with an Intel Xeon Gold 6154 CPU and an NVIDIA TITAN V GPU. The software environment consists of Python 3.10, CUDA 11.1 and PyTorch 1.7.0. During training, the Adam optimizer is adopted with an initial learning rate of 1e-4 and a batch size of 4, and the training process runs for 100 epochs. The training process is constrained by the 𝐿 𝑐𝑜𝑛 loss function defined in Section 3.5, and the mIoU metric is used to monitor model status and select the optimal model weights. 

4.2 Evaluation indicators 

In order to make a unified and quantifiable comparison of the segmentation ability of different algorithms, ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS this paper follows the common evaluation criteria in the field of medical image segmentation, and selects a variety of indicators to describe the performance from different perspectives, such as region overlap degree, volume consistency, and boundary approximation accuracy. Specifically, it includes mean intersection over Union (mIoU), Dice coefficient (Dice), Recall (Recall), Volume overlap error (VOE), relative volume difference (RVD), and Hausdorff distance (HD). The evaluation metrics used are shown in Table 1. In the calculation of the above metrics, the notational conventions involved are as follows: The pixel relationship between the true label and the model prediction is divided into four classes, namely, true positive (TP), true negative (TN), false positive (FP), and false negative (FN). Here, TP represents the set of pixels correctly identified by the model as lesions, and TN represents the part correctly identified as background or normal tissue. FP corresponds to the region where normal tissue is misdetected as a lesion, while FN represents the missed lesion pixels. In addition, P is the predicted target region, G is the real region manually labeled, and the symbol abs means taking the absolute value operation of the result. mIoU, Dice and Recall are positive metrics, and higher values indicate superior segmentation accuracy. VOE and RVD belong to error metrics, where lower original values correspond to smaller segmentation errors. To unify evaluation criteria, VOE and RVD are transformed by subtracting the original values from one. After transformation, VOE and RVD follow the same rule that higher values reflect better performance. A unified evaluation standard is thus established for mIoU, Dice, Recall, VOE and RVD, which facilitates horizontal comparison and experimental analysis of different algorithms. HD is adopted to quantify segmentation boundary deviation, and a lower HD value indicates more accurate segmentation of lesion edges.

4.3 Experimental analysis 

To fully validate the overall performance of the proposed multi-modal segmentation model, as well as the effectiveness of its core modules and loss function, comparative experiments, inter-module ablation experiments and intra-module ablation experiments are sequentially implemented in this section. Case-level five-fold cross-validation is adopted for dataset partitioning to eliminate biases resulting from data splitting. All experiments are performed on identical devices and operating environments following the configurations described in Section 4.1, so that consistent experimental conditions are guaranteed for all groups. 

4.3.1 Comparative experiment 

To systematically assess the effectiveness and stability of the proposed method, comparative experiments against ten mainstream single-modal and multi-modal segmentation networks are conducted on the multi-modal lung tumor dataset and the BraTS2019 brain tumor dataset. U-Net, SeResUnet, UTNet, MsTGANet, TransUnet and DconnNet are classified as single-modal segmentation networks, while MEAUNet, Guide-Ynet, Mdco-Unet and DBW-Unet are regarded as multi-modal segmentation networks. 5. Conclusion 

Existing multi-modal medical image segmentation methods usually model the segmentation task as a pixel-level independent classification problem, ignoring the topological information among pixels, and it is difficult to effectively integrate cross-modal spatial and frequency domain features from the perspective of topological relations, which limits the ability of the model to express complex lesion boundary structures. To solve the above problems, a cross-modal space-frequency interactive semantic segmentation network based on topological feature enhancement is proposed. From the dual perspectives of connectivity topology modeling and spatial-frequency feature fusion, the adjacency relationship and topological constraints among pixels are explicitly introduced, and three masks of connected region, lesion region and lesion edge are used for joint supervision, so that the network can simultaneously learn regional semantic information, boundary structure information and topological relationship among pixels. As a result, the model's ability to perceive the lesion boundary structure is significantly enhanced. Experimental results on two multi-modal medical image datasets show that the proposed method achieves significant improvement in multiple evaluation indicators. Compared with the baseline model, the mIoU, Dice, VOE, RVD and Recall of the proposed model are increased by 3.03%, 2.34%, 4.76%, 3.68% and 2.3% on the lung tumor dataset, respectively. On the brain tumor dataset, the mIoU, Dice, VOE, RVD and Recall of the proposed algorithm are increased by 3.69%, 2.93%, 6.23%, 4.37% and 4.06%, respectively. Among them, the improvements of mIoU and Dice show that the proposed method can recover the structural boundaries of the lesion region more accurately, thus reducing the local region rupture caused by edge blurring. The increase of VOE and RVD shows that the proposed model effectively reduces the overlap error and volume error between the predicted region and the true region, thus alleviating the over-segmentation or under-segmentation problem caused by ignoring topological information in traditional methods. The improvement of recall rate indicates that the proposed method has stronger lesion detection ability in low contrast regions and regions with complex contour structure, and reduces the risk of missed detection. Although TopoSFI-MUNet makes up for the shortcomings of traditional pixel-level modeling methods through the effective interaction of cross-modal connectivity topological features in the spatial and frequency domains, the network still has some limitations. In this paper, the pixel connectivity topological relationship is constructed by fixed neighborhood, which belongs to static topological modeling, and the topological receptive field cannot be adaptively adjusted according to the size of the lesion and the degree of contour distortion. For diffuse small lesions, the fixed neighborhood is easy to contain a large number of irrelevant background pixels, which introduces redundant connected topological association, weakens the topological feature representation ability of small lesions, and finally limits the generalization performance of the model in multi-modal clinical images with large lesion scale span. Therefore, the future work will focus on the optimization and improvement of ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS robust topological feature extraction, design a dynamic adaptive topological feature receptive field extraction module, adaptively adjust the neighborhood range based on the shape and size of the lesion, and further improve the adaptation ability of connectivity topological constraints to multi-scale and diffuse small lesions. In summary, TopoSFI-MUNet, a cross-modal spatial-frequency interactive semantic segmentation network based on topological feature enhancement proposed in this paper, not only realizes the effective interaction of cross-modal connectivity topological features in the spatial domain and frequency domain, but also significantly improves the segmentation ability of the model for complex lesion regions. At the same time, the existing shortcomings of the model and the targeted optimization direction are sorted out, which lays a research foundation for the subsequent exploration of more robust topological feature extraction schemes.
