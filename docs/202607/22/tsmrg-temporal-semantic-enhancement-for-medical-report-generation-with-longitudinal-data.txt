## Abstract:

Automatic medical report generation from radiology images is a challenge due to the need for precise disease identification and clinical understanding. Recently, a few approaches have been aware of the critical temporal effect for accurate analysis. However, how to leverage temporal clues has not been fully explored. To take full advantage of historical radiology images for per patient, a novel Temporal Semantic Framework for Medical Report Generation (TSMRG) is proposed: Firstly, a cascaded transformer module is proposed to aggregate the temporal and spatial clues of longitudinal chest X-ray images. Then, the hybrid contrastive alignment is designed to align the visual and textual features at both single and temporal levels. Finally, a diagnosis classification is introduced to guide report generation. Extensive experiments on MIMIC-CXR, longitudinal MIMIC-CXR, and MS-CXR-T datasets demonstrate that the proposed method could improve the quality of generated reports significantly. For example, TSMRG could achieve 0.398 F1 score in Longitudinal MIMIC-CXR dataset, and surpass the state-of-the-art (SOTA) approach by 10.3%. The code is available at https://github.com/xiaoxiu121/TSMRG.
