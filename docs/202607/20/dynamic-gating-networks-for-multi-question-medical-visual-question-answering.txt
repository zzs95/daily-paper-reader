Title: Dynamic Gating Networks for Multi-Question Medical Visual Question Answering

URL Source: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-981-92-3560-5_1

Published Time: 2027

Markdown Content:
## Abstract

Medical Visual Question Answering (Med-VQA) faces a critical dichotomy between inference efficiency and reasoning depth. While the Multi-Question Learning (MQL) paradigm significantly reduces computational redundancy via parallel processing, existing methods are fundamentally constrained by data-driven “shortcut learning,” lacking explicit medical decision bases and suffering from cross-modal misalignment when external reasoning texts are introduced. To bridge this gap, we propose Dynamic Reasoning Multi-Question Learning (Dr-MQL), a novel framework designed to simulate the clinical “Observation-Reasoning-Diagnosis” cognitive pathway. Specifically, we introduce a Multi-step Reasoning module to transform implicit medical knowledge into structured explicit representations via specialized attention mechanisms, thereby achieving significant reasoning enhancement for fine-grained pathological semantics. Furthermore, a Dynamic Gating Fusion (DGF) mechanism is designed to adaptively recalibrate confidence weights between visual evidence and textual priors, effectively mitigating semantic conflicts and noise interference during multi-modal fusion. Experiments on the SLAKE and PathVQA datasets demonstrate that Dr-MQL not only achieves superior accuracy but also exhibits clinician-like, “what-you-see-is-what-you-get” interpretable decision-making capabilities, as evidenced by visualization analysis.
