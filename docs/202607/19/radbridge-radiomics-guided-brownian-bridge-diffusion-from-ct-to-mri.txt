## Abstract

In acute ischemic stroke (AIS), MRI synthesis from readily available CT images can offer MRI-like soft-tissue contrast without requiring additional image acquisition. However, the limited sensitivity of CT to early ischemic lesions makes this task challenging, often leading to missing lesions, blurred boundaries, and unstable textures. This motivates the development of RadBridge, a radiomics-guided Brownian bridge diffusion framework that integrates radiomics priors with frequency-domain consistency. Specifically, we formulate MRI synthesis from CT as a two-dimensional Brownian bridge diffusion process, allowing the generated MRI to remain anatomically aligned with the input CT. Radiomics differences are used to localize lesion candidate regions, and the extracted priors are injected into the denoising process through conditional modulation to enhance lesion-aware representation. In addition, an ROI-based frequency-domain constraint is introduced to improve texture realism in lesion regions, and a lightweight terminal refinement strategy is applied to enhance local stability during inference. On ISLES2018, RadBridge obtains superior PSNR, SSIM, and lesion-region MI compared with representative medical image translation methods, and yields MRI results with more consistent anatomical structures and refined lesion-related textures.
