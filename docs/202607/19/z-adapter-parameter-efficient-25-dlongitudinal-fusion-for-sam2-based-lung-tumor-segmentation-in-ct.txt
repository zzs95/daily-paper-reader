Title: Z-Adapter: Parameter-Efficient 2.5DLongitudinal Fusion for SAM2-Based Lung Tumor Segmentation in CT

URL Source: https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-981-92-3420-2_33

Published Time: 2027

Markdown Content:
## Abstract

Promptable foundation models have accelerated medical image segmentation, yet naïvely applying 2D models slice-by-slice on CT often yields longitudinal flicker: unstable tumor masks across adjacent slices that degrade volumetric measurements and downstream planning. We propose Z-Adapter, a lightweight 2.5D adaptation that injects longitudinal context into a frozen SAM2-style encoder with minimal trainable parameters. Given a k-slice window, Z-Adapter performs depthwise 1D token mixing along the slice axis and updates only a small adapter and the mask decoder, while a boundary-aware longitudinal consistency loss suppresses inter-slice flicker without over-smoothing tumor edges. On lung tumor CT segmentation, Z-Adapter achieves 93.8% DSC and 91.5% slice consistency, outperforming competitive parameter-efficient baselines and approaching full 3D fine-tuning at a fraction of trainable parameters.
