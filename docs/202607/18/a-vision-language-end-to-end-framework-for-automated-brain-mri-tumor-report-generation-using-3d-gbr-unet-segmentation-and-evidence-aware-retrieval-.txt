Abstract 

Multimodal MRI faithful brain tumor segmentation and radiological reporting have not been 

achieved because of heterogeneous tumor morphology, low -contrast acquisitions, and the 

required coherent 3D volumetric interpretation across T1, T2 and FLAIR sequences. 

Traditional 3D deep learning (DL) architectures lack spatial continuity , whereas transformer -

based 3D models are computationally intensive and unreliable with small medical datasets. In 

addition, standalone Large Language Model (LLM) report generators tend to be visually 

ungrounded, whic h can lead to hallucinations . To overcome these shortcomings, offer Retrieval 

Augmented Visual Prompting (RAVP )-Brain, a joint DL and vision -language architecture that 

proposes automated brain tumor reporting across the entire 3D multimodal MRI. The system 

includes (1) a 3D CNN -LSTM classifier, (2) a novel Global Bottleneck Refiner UNet (GBR 

UNet) to robustly segment tumor s, and (3) a n ovel RAVP to generate clinically meaningful 

reports. GBR UNet utilises lightweight transformer -based global refiners in the bottleneck 

pathway to employ geometry -aware 3D convolutions, thereby achieving better tumor 

delineation, boundary preservation and long range context modelling than classical UNet s and 

transformer heavy models. RAVP recalls Top K visually similar MRI cases and injects the 

multi -level visual prompts, scan level, entity level and neighbour case level, in Groq 

accelerated Llama models, significantly reducing hallucinations and enhancing diagnostic 

consistency . Assessment of the BraTS -Africa dataset demonstrates that RAVP -Brain offers 

higher segmentation accuracy, stable volumetric classification, and clinical structure reporting. 

The suggested pipeline, which not only separate s 3D medical imaging from LLM -based 

reasoning, but also provides an explainable, end -to -end solution for automated analysis of brain 

tumors .

Keywords: Report Generation, Retrieval Augmented Generation, 3D Deep Learning, 3D Brain 

MRI Segmentation, Large Language Models , Medical Imaging .1.  Introduction 

The diagnosis of brain tumor s is one of the most challenging issues in neuro -oncology because 

it involves extremely heterogeneous morphological patterns that manifest differently across 

patients, scanners, and MRI acquisition protocols [1] [2] . To obtain an accurate interpretation, 

radiologists should analyze a number of 3D MRI sequences, including T1 -weighted, T2 -

weighted, and FLAIR, as well as contrast -enhanced T1C, to evaluate anatomical structures, 

edema, distribution, necrotic areas, and e nhancement properties [3], [4] . Nevertheless, this is a 

time -consuming manual process subject to inter -observer variability and limited by the global 

supply of trained radiologists [5] . DL has demonstrated potential in the automation of brain 

tumor analysis, although there are still a number of limitations. Traditional 3D CNN classifiers 

limit the heterogeneity of tumor s, lack generalizability across institutions, and exhibit domain 

shifts [6] . The classical UNet models remain the most common in segmentation . H owever, they 

lack receptive fields and fail to model long -range relationships, leading to inaccurate tumor 

boundaries in low -contrast or noisy MRI data [7] , [8] . Transformer -based segmentation 

networks also employ global context modelling, but they require large datasets and are 

computationally expensive, making them difficult to apply in a clinical setting [8], [9] . Besides, 

the majority of previous 2D slice -based models are not volume -continuity, and thus, they 

exhibit multimodal misalignment and different predictions across slices[10] , [11]. 

To address these issues, the proposed system suggested a new GBR UNet, a hybrid 3D 

segmentation architecture, local spatial encoding with CNNs and global refinement with 

lightweight transformers in the bottleneck pathway. This two -path design leads to incr eased 

accuracy in boundary estimates, increased multimodal MR image sensitivity, and increased 

resistance to multimodal UNet variants, in comparison to transformer -heavy models that tend 

to be unstable [12] , [13]. 

After the segmentation of the brain tumor, the proposed model was designed to generate 

clinically coherent radiology reports. When the generation report by using the existing model 

faces some challenges, the proposed model using LLMs can produce fluent text and 

nevertheless, it usually ha s hallucinations of medical data without evidence of imaging [14]. It 

is demonstrated that such errors can be minimized by Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG), 

which grounds the reasoning process on real -life similar cases [15]. Based on these 

observations, we contribute a RAVP -Brain model that integrates 

• 3D CNN -LSTM tumor classification, • The novel GBR UNet voxel tumor subregion delineation segmentation model, 

• Top K similar case retrieval -based evidence grounding, and 

• Radiology schema informed RAVP to produce clinically aligned radiology reports. 

A combination of these parts builds an interpretable, end -to -end system that 

automatically diagnoses and reports brain tumor s.

2.  Related Works 

Multimodal MRI streamlining, volumetric convolutional networks, and transformer -powered 

vision -language systems have improved the state of the art in DL -based brain tumor analysis 

and the automation of radiology reports is an i nherent challenge . Standardised benchmarks 

with the BRATS challenges made the segmentation of heterogeneous tumor subregions across 

multimodal MRI sequences (T1, T2, FLAIR, and contrast -enhanced images) intrinsically 

difficult, which drove the creation of architectures capable of capturing complete spatial 

context [1], [3] . The initial CNN -based multimodal pipelines demonstrated that volumetric 

fusion is a highly effective method for tumor delineation compared to some existing designed 

techniques , especially when there is spatial continuity between modalities [4] .

Modern 3D tumor analysis is based on classical volumetric segmentation architectures. 

The 3D UNet proposed an encoder -decoder architecture that uses skip connections to perform 

volumetric dense segmentation with sparse annotations [5] . VNet followed this paradigm by 

applying residual blocks and Dice -loss -based optimization to enhance convergence and 

stability of segmentation in volumetric tumor datasets [10] . Further UNet variants ( ResUNet 

and R2 UNet families) improved gradient flow and contextual feature learning and allowed a 

better generalization to a variety of tumor morphologies [7], [11] [12] . Lightweight CNN 

extensions , a ttention -based CNN extensions were extensions of skip connections that included 

attention gates, trained to selectively highlight tumor relevant regions and suppress background 

activations to enhance localization of subregions such as enhancing tumor (ET), tumor core 

(TC), and peritumoral edema (WT), without global self -attention mechanisms [13] .

More recently, hybrid architectures that utilise transformers have added global self -

attention to explicitly learn long -range spatial dependencies that are beyond the reach of 

conventional CNNs [14] . TransUNet demonstrated that using transformer encoders with UNet 

decoders is effective for jointly modelling global context and local spatial detail [15] [16] . Later 

versions, such as axial and factorised attention -based architectures, such as AFTer -UNet, 

continued to investigate efficient transformer architectures in volumetric medical imaging [17] [18] . These developments , in particular, encourage the transformer -inspired 

bottleneck refinement policy utilised in the proposed GBR UNet , which combines 

convolutional volumetric characteristics with global contextual representations to achieve 

strong segmentation of non -homogeneous subregions of brain tumor s. 

Radiology report generation has advanced to the next phase, utilising CNN -RNN 

pipelines and LLM -based systems that can generate multi -sentence text with coherent, 

diagnostic content [19] . Biomedical LLMs show good clinical reasoning but are likely to have 

hallucinations when the evidence based on images is weak [20] . RAG addresses this problem 

by adding retrieved samples or formatted clinical knowledge to the LLM process and enhances 

consistent factual aspects and minimizes unsubstantiated assertions [21], [22] .

Complementarily, our pipeline RAVP module integrates Top K similar -case retrieval with 

multi -level visual prompting, including scan -, entity -, and neighbour -case -level features, which 

are fed to Groq -accelerated Llama models. The presented method , a retrieval -augmented visual 

prompting approach grounded in existing evidence from actual MRI studies, ensures that the 

generated radiology reports are faithful to the data and significantly reduces the likelihood of 

hallucinations, albeit at the cost of produci ng clinically consistent, structured radiology reports. 

The directions given by radiology societies, including the European Society of Radiology, 

focus on the organization of reporting systems, interpretability, and AI systems that assist 

radiologists without risk to the safety of patients [23] . These guidelines influenced the schema -

based prompting approach in RAVP Brain. 

The proposed GBR UNet lies between these classical and transformer -enhanced 

models. It shares 3D UNet and VNet volumetric processing and skip connection design, as well 

as Residual and recurrent connection stability, and attention -like mechanisms for selective 

refinement. Applying a transformer -based global refiner at the GBR UNet provides dual -

representation fusion, combining local 3D geometry and long -range tumor context, resolving 

the shortcomings of CNN - and transformer -based segmentation models. To complement this, 

RAVP ensures that the report generation process is evidence -based, interpretable, and clinically 

consistent. In real -world pipelines like nn UNet , it is stressed that preprocessing, spacing, 

patching, and hyperparameter optimization are equally significant in the role of architectural 

decisions in high -performance volumetric segmentation [14]. A combination of these design 

principles guides the construction of the 3D CNN -LSTM, GBR UNet , and RAVP -Brain 

system, an end -to -end , interpretable system for volumetric tumor classification, segmentation, 

and automated radiology report generation [24], [25], [26] .Based on these works, the next section provides a description of the proposed RAVP -Brain 

framework and explains how volumetric classification, global context -aware tumor 

segmentation, RAVP and schema -constrained language generation can be combined to 

generate clinically grounded, interpretable brain MRI reports. 

3.  An Evidence -aware RAVP -Brain Pipeline for Automated Brain MRI Report 

Generation Framework 

The proposed RAVP -Brain architecture is a vision -language framework, which is an end -

to -end , to analyze brain MRI tumors and generate radiology report. The system comprises four 

closely related modules: a volumetric tumor analyser, a semantic segmentation component, a 

similarity -based retrieval system, and a schema constrained language generator . Contrary to 

the traditional modular pipelines, where the classification, segmentation, and report generation 

processes are loosely coupled, it is noted that all el ements in RAVP -Brain are highly 

interrelated, which means that in this case, diagnostic decisions and created textual descriptions 

are always based on visual and quantitative imaging evidence. Driven by shortcomings of 

existing volumetric segmentation and radiology reporting pipelines, especially the inability to 

provide a global context of reasoning and ground report in evidence, the proposed RAVP -Brain 

represents a single diagnostic pipeline that closely integrates global context sensitive 3D tumor 

segmen tation with retrieval enhanced, schema constrained report generation. This architecture 

will ensure that the results of segmentation, quantitative measurements of the tumors , as well 

as the retrieved clinical evidence , will all feed the language model and consequently reduce the 

unsupported inferences and hallucinations.

3.1. Dataset Description 

The proposed work is based on the BraTS -Africa dataset, which is an extension of Brain Tumor 

Segmentation (BraTS) project that focuses on a particular clinical environment of Sub -Saharan 

Africa [30]. It is also characterized by multi -institutional MRI scans which were acquired on 

variable field -strength scanners and various imaging protocols and reflects real -world 

heterogeneity of clinical imaging quality in the region . Four standard MRI modalities are 

provided for each patient volume (T1n, T1c, T2 -weighte d, and T2 -FLAIR) in NIfTI (.nii) 

format, as well as tumor masks created by experts to identify and delineate three major tumor 

subregions: ET, ED, and NCR/NET components. Ground -truth labels are coded according to 

the standard encoding scheme of the BraTS of {0,1,2,4} that is popularly used in brain tumor 

segmentation studies [1]. 

It contains the dataset of patients diagnosed with Gliomas and Other Neoplasms, with the 

opportunity to perform the tasks of multi -class segmentation and binary -level tumor 

classification. Trained radiologists created the annotations according to the accep ted BraTS 

labelling guidelines, and the supervision of 3D DL models is of high quality [27] [28] . The 

variety of scanner types, appearance of tumors, and irregularity in the acquisition process make BraTS -Africa a complicated and realistic test set to assess strong segmentation, classification, 

and radiological report formation models. Experiments on the BraTS -Africa dataset show that 

multilabel segmentation performance improves, volumetric classification remains stable, and 

radiology reports are clinically consistent compared to traditional DL and LLM pipelines.

3. 4 GBR UNet for Tumor Segmentation 

GBR UNet is a mix of these two concepts to grant accuracy and efficiency. The first one is that 

a conventional 3D UNet encoder is trained to learn local tumor characteristics, including edges, 

textures, and boundaries. On the bottom level of the network, a global bottleneck refiner is 

added. This refiner has two parts: (i) a convolutional path that maintains accurate spatial 

information, and (ii ) a lightweight transformer branch, which extracts long -range relationships 

throughout the tumor volume. 

Figure 2 shows the proposed GBR UNet , a modification of the 3D UNet that includes a 

GBR on the final layer. It is an encoder -bottleneck -decoder network with residual and long 

skip -connections to provide spatial consistency. The left in the diagram denotes the 3D UNet 

encoder , which reduces the input MRI volume in successive steps to gain local features 

(hierarchical). All the encoding stages include 3D convolutional layers with normalization and 

non -linear activation. Remaining ties are incorporated to allow the gradient flow and 

stabilization of training. The layers have been effective in maintaining fine -grained spatial 

characteristics such as tumor boundaries, texture variations and local intensity variations. At 

the minimum resolution level, it is replaced by a GBR that does not directly give features to 

the decoder. This module has two parallel branches , each with a CNN bottleneck and a 

Transformer bottleneck. This dual -branch design aims to retain local spatial accuracy while 

incorporating global contextual reasoning.

Unlike TransUNet architectures that spread transformer modules across different encoder 

stages, the proposed GBR UNet takes an entirely different approach, placing a lightweight 

transformer at the bottleneck. This location ensures a high spatial inductive bias for 

convolutional layers while greatly reducing computational complexity. GRB -UNet can be 

particularly useful for 3D medical image segmentation with limited data and computing 

resources because it can identify long -range dependencies between semantic representations 

without compromising boundary sharpness or training stability by applying self -attention 

exclusively to the high -level semantic representations .

In contrast to previous hybrid architecture designs that place transformer blocks within 

the encoder hierarchy, the proposed framework introduces a Global Bottleneck Refiner that 

comprises parallel convolutional and lightweight transformer branches. This module works 

only at the most semantic level, the level of the global contextual reasoning the most 

meaningful, as well as computationally efficient. Instead of spreading attention mechanis ms 

across the network, GBR UNet implements local morphological refin ement and global 

structural modelling, partitioned and controlled, with semantic grounding .

This bottleneck hybridisation is not just a minimi zation of transformer consumption, 

but a planned architectural redesign aimed at receptive field optimisation , inductive bias 

maintenance and 3D volumetric scalability. The proposed approach improves optimisation 

stability, minimises memory consumption, and reduces the risk of overfitting in small medical 

datasets by supporting a fully convolutional encoder -decoder backbone and confining attention 

to a small bottleneck representation. To the best of our unde rstanding, a compartmentalised, efficiency -minded integration of global awareness within a three -dimensional framework of 

tumor segmentation has not been systematically explored in earlier TransUNet -calibre 

strategies .

Moreover, the learnt representations are used to generate automated radiology reports. 

The transformer -derived global features capture the overall tumor , whereas segmentation 

masks provide accurate volumetric and spatial values. This will ensure that the resulting 

diagnostic reports are directly based on measurable evidence from the imaging , thereby 

enhancing the clinical interpretability of the suggested framework. 

3.5 Retrieval -Augmented Visual Prompting (RAVP) for Radiology Report Generation 

While accurate tumor segmentation provides precise quantitative delineation of tumor 

subregions, clinically meaningful radiology reporting requires structured reasoning grounded 

in visual evidence and prior clinical knowledge. To satisfy this need, we propose RAVP, a

conditioning LLM that uses multi -level visual, quantitative, and contextual cues derived from 

imaging data and other relevant cases . The generated prompt is sent to Groq Llama -3.3 -70B 

(SpecDec) to generate a report. The LLM is limited to adheri ng to a predefined clinical 

reporting schema, which ensures uniform, clinically significant results. The report that is 

generated comprises Findings, Tumor Characteristics, Impression, and Recommendations, 

which is similar to the typical radiology process.

4.  Results and Discussion 

In this section, a full analysis of the proposed GBR UNet and RAVP -Brain models will be 

presented, covering all four features: segmentation accuracy, boundary precision, learning 

stability, and report -level clinical coherence. Table 1 presents quantitative comparisons 

between the proposed architecture and existing CNN - or Transformer -based architectures ,

followed by a qualitative visual evaluation and analysis of report generation . Discussions focus 

on results based on both technical performance and clinical relevance to achieve a balanced, 

evidence -based interpr etation. 

4.1 Quantitative Segmentation Performance 

As shown in Table 1, the comparative evaluation of brain tumor segmentation models 

demonstrates that the proposed hybrid GBR UNet consistently outperforms existing CNN - and 

Transformer -based architectures. A detailed comparison of various 3D brain tumor 

segmentation models based on Dice, IoU , and HD95 metrics in the Whole WT, TC and ET 

regions is provided in the table. Traditional 3D UNet achieved Dice scores of 0.69 (WT), 0.74 (TC), and 0.71 (ET). Hybrid models such as ResUNet3D and TransUNet3D improved TC and 

ET Dice to 0.83 –0.84 and 0.80 –0.82, respectively. In comparison, the proposed GBR UNet 

achieved Dice scores of 0.80 ± 0.015 (WT), 0.88 ± 0.012 (TC), and 0.86 ± 0.014 (ET), 

representing consistent improvements across all subregions. Similarly, IoU values increased 

to 0.67 (WT), 0.79 (TC), and 0.75 (ET), indicating stronger volumetric agreement. Boundary 

precision, as measured by HD95, was reduced to 5.8 mm (WT), 6.0 mm (TC) , and 6.4 mm 

(ET), compared to 9.8 –11.4 mm observed in baseline models. 

These gains indicate that the dual -bottleneck fusion approach is a successful way to combine 

convolutional local refinement and transformer -based global context modelling while 

simultaneously maintaining CNN inductive bias. This architecture mitigates the overfitting 

behaviour of pure transformer hybrids with limited medical data. Clinically, better boundary 

accuracy and volumetric consistency can help provide more accurate tumor burden estimation 

and longitudinal response, but will need to be prospectively validated. 

GRB -UNet achieves its performance gains through its dual -bottleneck fusion architecture, 

combining convolutional volumetric characteristics with transformer -refined global context 

while retaining the CNN inductive bias, thereby eliminating the overfitting commonly seen 

with pure transformer hybrids on small medical datasets. This has a clinical implication : a 

better, more consistent definition of tumor subregions to facilitate accurate estimation of tumor 

burden, as well as increased confidence in radiologi cal reporting, treatment planning, and 

assessing tumor progression.

preserved to a considerable extent. The evaluation at clinical concept level registered a 

precision, recall and F1 -score of 0.50, which indicates the correct identification of significant 

results like lesion enhancement, location and type of tumor. 

The moderate ROUGE scores reflect a structural disparity between brief reports and 

reference summaries, as well as between schema -expanded generated reports. In contrast to 

template -copying systems, the framework suggested generates structured context -enriched 

reports with volumetric quantification and retrieval -based validation. Notably, the model is 

diagnostic cautious , ensuring diagnostics are performed only when necessary, minimising the 

risk of false claims. These findings imply that the developed RAVP -Brain model develops 

clinically consistent reports without unfounded hallucinations .

4. 5 Integrated Interpretation 

Taken together, the findings show that increased segmentation faithfulness is associated with 

higher report -level semantic coherence. Visual embeddings seem to be generated more reliably 

downstream, and this is structurally based. Global -context segmentation, retrieval -based 

contextualisation, and schema -constrained LLM prompting make a unified pipeline to AI -

assisted neuroimaging reporting. (a) 

Figure 5 (a) and 5(b) illustrates the integration of 3D GBR UNet segmentation, case -based 

retrieval, and report generation enabled by LLMs into the proposed RAVP -Brain framework to 

improve clinical reporting. Although the radiologist's note briefly describes a questionable 

enhancing lesion, the generated report retains the diagnostic purpose of the note and extends it 

into a structured MRI report, with quantitative tumor volume estimation, standardised 

localisation to anatomical areas, and clinically appropriate recommendations .(b) 

Figure 5(a) and 5(b) RAVP -Brain framework report generation results 

The retrieval module also enhances interpretability by citing very similar historical 

cases that allow contextual validation of the results, and no unsupported conclusions are 

asserted. Notably, the LLM is cautious in its diagnosis, suggesting histopathological 

confirmation, which is consistent with real -world radiology practice. This demonstrates how 

RAVP -Brain can convert sparse expert observations into detailed , clinically understandable 

reports while retaining semantic fidelity and patient safety. RAVP -Brain uses 3D segmentation, 

retrieval -based context, and LLM thinking to transform succinct radiologist notes into 

quantitative, structured, and clinically aligned MRI reports. However, at the moment, evaluation is only restricted to the retrospective validation of a

single dataset . Further assessment of generalisability and deployment feasibility is needed, 

using external multi -institutional testing and prospective clinical workflow studies. Overall, 

the suggested framework illustrates good performance, interpretability, and correspondence 

with radiological reporting standards in the analyzed context. 

Generally, experimental findings indicate that the recommended GBR UNet steadily 

improves volumetric overlap and boundary accuracy, whereas the RAVP -Brain framework has 

the potential to enhance structured clinical reporting through segmentation -based reasoning 

and context integration facilitated by retrieval. The above features of quantitative accuracy, 

stable training behaviour, and clinically consistent report generation suggest that the proposed 

methodology would provide a stable platform for AI -assisted neuroimaging processes. This 

indicates that segmentation, retrieval and LLM -based reasoning can be utilised in conjunction 

with radiological interpretation without semantic fidelity or diagnostic prudence. Overall, the 

results imply that global -context segmentation, case -based retrieval, and structured LLM 

prompting are useful for improving anatomical delineation and generating reports useful in 

clinical practice . Although multi -institutional validation would be more appropriate, the 

proposed framework is robust, interpretable, and well aligned with radiological reporting 

standards .

The conventional clinical activity involves radiologists who manually estimate the size of 

the tumor in MRI slices, and manually prepare narrative reports. Not only is this time -

consuming , but it is also prone to inter -observer variability , especially when volumetric 

determination is inferred from 2D slices. This intend s to overcome these shortcomings by 

proposing the RAVP -Brain framework, which is a fully end -to -end system of AI -driven 

processing that incorporates 3D segmentation of tumor, multi -class clas sification of tumor, 

volume quantification of tumor, and the generation of structured medical reports. The 

framework offers more credible tumor burden estimation by using volumetric MRI analysis as 

opposed to slice -by -slice estimation. Moreover, this is because the combination of structured 

medical reporting schemas with a n LLM guarantees clinically interpretable and standardized 

results, and thus minimizes reporting variability and cognitive load. 

The proposed work demonstrates good performance but can still be improved. Future 

directions will be to incorporate other MRI modalities to enhance the characterization of 

tumor s, to track longitudinal progression of treatments, to integrate clinical metadata to provide personalised decision support, and to provide large -scale multi -centre validation to guarantee 

relevance. Furthermore, enhancing explainability systems and streamlining implementation in 

hospital Picture Archiving and Communication System (PACS ) settings will be some of the 

necessary phases towards real -world clinical translation. These extensions will enhance the 

power, interpretability and clinical applicability of the proposed RAVP -Brain framework. 

5.  Conclusions and Future Work 

This paper introduces case -based retrieval and LLM -driven report generation based on 3D 

GBR UNet segmentation of 3D cases into RAVP -Brain, a single framework that aids the 

generation of accurate, clinically readable brain MRI reports. Quantitative segmentation 

outcomes indicate that the proposed GBR UNet is always superior to current CNN and 

transformer -based models . Report -level analysis reveals high semantic performance, clinically 

significant entity detection, and safe diagnostic expression despite moder ate lexical overlap. 

The qualitative analysis also substantiates that RAVP -Brain is effective at converting 

radiologist -generated brief reports into structured, quantitative reports, with the added 

advantage of case -based reasoning, free of hallucinations generated by unsupported systems .

Future research will aim to achieve multi -institutional validation on a large scale, incorporate 

radiologist -in -the -loop feedback, expand to longitudinal and multimodal imaging conditions, 

and improve clinical concept eval uation to strengthen external validity and applicability in real 

clinical settings.
