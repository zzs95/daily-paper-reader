## Abstract:

Automatic and accurate segmentation of the left atrium (LA) from late gadolinium-enhanced cardiac magnetic resonance (LGE CMR) images is crucial for atrial fibrillation (AF) management. However, data privacy and heterogeneity pose challenges to collaborative research. Although federated learning (FL) enables collaborative training preserving privacy across institutions, heterogeneous feature distributions and labeling variations significantly impact segmentation performances. To address these issues, we propose FedAP, an Adaptive Personalized Federated Learning framework, to address feature and label heterogeneity while achieving personalized LA segmentation. FedAP integrates a Multi-Center Low-Frequency Fusion (MCFF) network for cross-center feature alignment by aligning low-frequency amplitude spectra, a Distribution Prototype-based Uncertainty Learning (DPUL) network to explicitly model annotation-related uncertainty, and an Adaptive Local Refinement (ALR) network for personalized prediction. Experiments on multi-center LA datasets demonstrate state-of-the-art performance of FedAP.

### 1. INTRODUCTION

Automatic and accurate segmentation of the left atrium (LA) from late gadolinium-enhanced cardiac magnetic resonance images (LGE CMR) is crucial for the diagnosis and treatment of atrial fibrillation (AF) [1], [2]. AF is a common arrhythmia and its occurrence and development are closely related to structural remodeling of the LA [3]. Clinically, evaluating structural remodeling of the LA serves as an important basis for formulating personalized treatment plans and predicting treatment outcomes [4]. Accurate quantification of the structure of the LA relies on the precise segmentation of the left atrium. Leveraging its high soft-tissue resolution and the enhanced contrast it provides, LGE CMR enables effective assessment of left atrial fibrosis and structural remodeling through left atrial segmentation [5]. However, current clinical practice still relies primarily on physicians for manual segmentation, a tedious and time-consuming process that is significantly influenced by subjective factors. Therefore, the development of automated high-precision left atrial segmentation techniques based on LGE CMR images has become an urgent need to improve the quality of AF diagnosis and treatment.
