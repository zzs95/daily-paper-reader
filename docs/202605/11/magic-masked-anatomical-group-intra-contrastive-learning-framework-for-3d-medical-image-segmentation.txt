## Abstract:

In 3D medical image segmentation, traditional self-supervised methods often rely on low-level color semantics such as grayscale or texture, which are susceptible to imaging parameters and noise. Consequently, contrast learning based on spatial position has emerged as a more robust approach. However, current position-based contrast learning still faces three major challenges: limited spatial context, lack of hierarchical modeling, and high computational load. To address these issues, inspired by the group-reduction of Rubik’s Cube, this article proposes Masked Anatomical Group Intra Contrastive Learning Framework (Magic). The Magic framework consists of two key components: (1) a Hierarchical Positional Contrast Module, which divides anatomical groups using prior knowledge, builds hierarchical positive and negative samples, and models spatial relationships across anatomical scales via a coarse-to-fine contrast strategy to guide the model to focus on effective spatial representations; and (2) a Group-reduction Masking Strategy, which uses group-theoretic partitioning to form multi-scale subgroups and applies hierarchical masking with parallel reconstruction to enrich cross-patch contextual information. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Magic achieves superior segmentation results compared to existing state-of-the-art methods across multiple 3D medical image datasets, while requiring only approximately 4 GB of GPU memory, indicating strong potential for clinical deployment.

### 1. INTRODUCTION

3D medical image segmentation aims to distinguish organs, tissues, or pathological voxels within images. Since deep learning training relies heavily on manually annotated data, and 3D annotation is both time-consuming and labor-intensive with scarce annotated datasets, while abundant unlabeled clinical images remain underutilized, self-supervised learning (SSL) has emerged as a key direction for enhancing 3D image representation capabilities [1]. SSL enables the extraction of intrinsic structural information from images using unlabeled data, thereby improving downstream segmentation performance [2].
