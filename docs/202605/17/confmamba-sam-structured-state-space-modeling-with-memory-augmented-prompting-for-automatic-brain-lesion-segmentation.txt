## Abstract:

Accurate and consistent brain lesion segmentation from clinical MRI and CT volumes remains challenging due to microscale lesions, low contrast, anisotropic resolution, and interslice discontinuities. To address these issues, we propose ConfMamba-SAM, an end-to-end, fully automatic segmentation framework that leverages a frozen foundation model backbone with lightweight, trainable adapters for efficient adaptation. The ConfMamba encoder introduces a structured 3×3 corner→edge→center scanning mechanism within a state space model, prioritizing central regions and reinforcing directional features along anatomical axes—enhancing sensitivity to small and low-contrast lesions. To enable interaction-free prompting, a memory-driven prompt generator maintains a learnable prototype bank across adjacent slices, synthesizing structural prompts with temporal coherence. A multi-scale, memory-augmented decoder progressively refines segmentation masks under deep supervision, selectively attending to historical feature states to preserve boundary fidelity and ensure 3D consistency. Evaluated on a composite benchmark including BraTS2021, ISLES2022, FCD2023, ICH2020, and Instance2022, ConfMamba-SAM achieves state-of-the-art performance in both accuracy and robustness, while maintaining high computational efficiency.

### 1. Introduction

Accurate brain lesion segmentation from clinical MRI and CT underpins diagnosis, therapy assessment, and surgical planning. However, lesions are often small, low-contrast, and bounded by fuzzy or irregular edges, compounded by anisotropic resolution, multi-center domain shifts, and limited annotations. These factors challenge fine-grained discrimination and cross-slice consistency—especially in thick or unevenly spaced volumes.
