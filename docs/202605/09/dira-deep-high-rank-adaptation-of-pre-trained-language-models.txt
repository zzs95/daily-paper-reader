## Abstract:

A common practice in fine-tuning large language models is to freeze the weights of the original pre-trained model while optimizing a low-rank adapter. However, existing adapters typically rely on single-layer networks, whereas empirical evidence suggests that deeper networks can extract more comprehensive information. To address this, we propose Deep High-Rank Adaptation(DiRA) to capture richer, more nuanced information. Drawing inspiration from the U-Net architecture, we develop a funnel-shaped deep adapter. We employ Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) initialization and freezing of high-rank matrices A and B to reduce trainable parameters by 10× compared to Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA). Our method has effectively decreased GPU memory usage. Experiments on LLaMA3-8B across eight commonsense reasoning datasets show that DiRA achieves 85.4% average accuracy with only 3.74M trainable parameters, demonstrating superior parameter-performance trade-offs. Our source codes are publicly available at https://github.com/marcocst/DiRA.

### 1. INTRODUCTION

In recent years, large-scale pre-trained models (LPMs) have achieved remarkable breakthroughs in natural language processing [1], [2]. Their success primarily stems from leveraging massive datasets and ever-increasing parameter scales, demonstrating powerful representation and generalization capabilities. As the parameter scale of large models grows, the computational cost of training them for downstream tasks increases significantly [3].
