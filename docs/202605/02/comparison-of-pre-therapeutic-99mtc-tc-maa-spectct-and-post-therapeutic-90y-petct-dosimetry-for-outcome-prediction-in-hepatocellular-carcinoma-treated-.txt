Title: Comparison of pre-therapeutic [99mTc]Tc-MAA SPECT/CT and post-therapeutic 90Y-PET/CT dosimetry for outcome prediction in hepatocellular carcinoma treated with resin microspheres

URL Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00259-026-07903-5

Markdown Content:
## Abstract

### Purpose

To compare pre- and post-therapy dosimetry in association with clinical outcomes in a large cohort of hepatocellular carcinoma treated with yttrium 90 (90 Y) labelled resin-microspheres.

### Materials and methods

This was a retrospective study from a single centre of data collected (146 patients) between March 2015 and October 2019. Pre-therapeutic tumour-absorbed doses was computed using technetium 99m (99m Tc) macroaggregated human albumin SPECT/CT. Post therapeutic tumour-absorbed dose was computed using 90 Y PET/CT. The mRECIST response criteria at 6 months was correlated to the tumour-absorbed doses by receiver-operator-curve (ROC) analysis and disease control probability modelling. Overall survival was stratified according to the results of the ROC analysis.

### Results

One hundred and fourteen (114) patients (median age 67.5, 89 men) were evaluated in the study. Objective response was observed in 40% of patients and 27% had stable disease. Median pre/post-therapeutic tumour-absorbed doses were respectively 126 Gy (IQR: 104-165 Gy) and 84 Gy (IQR: 65-116 Gy) for objective response vs. 107 Gy (IQR: 68-141 Gy) and 71 Gy (IQR: 52-80 Gy) for stable disease and 102 Gy (IQR: 61-138 Gy) and 56 Gy (IQR: 43-78 Gy) for progressive disease (_p_=0.039 and _p_<0.001). On post-therapeutic dosimetry, disease control probability at 70 Gy was of 78% (95% CI : 46-94). Tumour absorbed doses over 70 Gy leaded to median overall survival respectively of 20 months (95% CI: 18-32).

### Conclusion

In our cohort, a pre-therapeutic [99m Tc]Tc-MAA SPECT/CT tumour absorbed dose ≥100 Gy and a post-therapeutic 90 Y PET/CT tumour absorbed dose ≥70 Gy were linked to improved disease control and survival. A linear correlation was found between pre- and post-therapeutic dosimetry for the largest tumours. Post-therapeutic 90 Y PET/CT–based dosimetry aids in treatment verification, quality assurance, and understanding dose–response relationships.
