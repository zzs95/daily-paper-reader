## Abstract

The combination of medical imaging, pathology reports, EHR, and radiological stories is the keystone of clinical decision-making. This paper provides a systematic analysis of multimodal fusion models on clinical Vision-language Models (VLM), early, late, intermediate, cross-modal attention, and hybrid systems based on radiology, pathology, dermatology, and ophthalmology (MIMIC-CXR, PadChest, TCGA-PATH, HAM10000) benchmarks. Adaptive gating and cross-modal attention were better than baselines by 4.7–11.3% percent on clinical VQA tasks at a minimal computational cost. Among the identified gaps are federated fusion learning, dynamic modality weighting, and interpretable fusion mechanisms, and practical architectural recommendations have been offered on what would be implemented as clinically deployable systems utilizing multimodal systems.
