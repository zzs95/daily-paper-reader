## Abstract 

Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) has emerged as a promis-ing paradigm for advancing complex reasoning in large language models, and recent work extends RLVR to multimodal large language models (MLLMs). This transfer, however, surfaces a faithfulness challenge: faithful perception of task-relevant visual evidence and faithful use of that evidence during reasoning, leading to unsatisfactory gains on multimodal benchmarks. Specifically, existing percep-tion supervision often operates on textual descriptions rather than natively on image regions, and faithful use is largely overlooked, exposing the perception–reasoning disconnect where correctly perceived evidence is dropped or contradicted during reasoning. To close these gaps, we propose Faithful-MR1 , a training framework that anchors and reinforces visual attention to address both halves of faithful multimodal reasoning. The Anchoring stage turns perception into an explicit pre-reasoning subtask, supervising a dedicated <Focus> token’s attention directly against image regions rather than through textual descriptions. The Reinforcing stage exposes faithful use through counterfactual image intervention, rewarding answer-correct trajectories that concentrate visual attention where vision causally matters. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Faithful-MR1 outperforms re-cent multimodal reasoning baselines on both Qwen2.5-VL-Instruct 3B and 7B backbones while using substantially less training data. 

## 1 Introduction 

Reinforcement learning with verifiable rewards (RLVR) has emerged as a promising paradigm for advancing complex reasoning in large language models [Guo et al., 2025, Shao et al., 2024, Jaech et al., 2024, Team et al., 2025, Lu et al., 2026]. Motivated by this success, a growing line of work extends RLVR to multimodal large language models (MLLMs), aiming to bring similar reasoning improvements to vision-language tasks [Huang et al., 2026b, Meng et al., 2025, Chen et al., 2025, Shen et al., 2025, Liu et al., 2025b, Yang et al., 2025, Xu et al., 2025a]. On multimodal reasoning benchmarks, however, this transferred recipe yields substantially smaller gains than in the text-only setting. Unlike text-only reasoning, a multimodal reasoning problem requires both faithful perception of task-relevant visual evidence from the image and faithful use of that evidence in the reasoning chain. Recent work addresses parts of these requirements: Perception-R1 [Xiao et al., 2026] and Vision-SR1 [Li et al., 2026] introduce supervision that rewards faithful visual descriptions, and VPPO [Huang et al., 2026a] reweights policy updates toward visually pivotal tokens. 2.1 RLVR for Multimodal Reasoning 

Reinforcement Learning with Verifiable Rewards (RLVR) has become a powerful paradigm for improving language-model reasoning in domains where final answers can be checked automati-cally [Guo et al., 2025, Jaech et al., 2024, Team et al., 2025, Tian et al., 2026]. RLVR is typically instantiated on top of PPO [Schulman et al., 2017]; its group-relative variant GRPO [Shao et al., 2024] replaces per-sample value estimation with group-based advantage normalization, and DAPO [Yu et al., 2025] further refines GRPO with techniques that stabilize training at scale. Recent work adapts this paradigm to multimodal large language models (MLLMs) by defining verifiable rewards over image-text tasks. Vision-R1 [Huang et al., 2026b] demonstrates the effectiveness of R1-style answer-level RLVR for multimodal reasoning, and a broad line of follow-up work explores rule-based RL across diverse task families, training scales, rollout strategies, and shortcut-mitigation designs [Meng et al., 2025, Chen et al., 2025, Shen et al., 2025, Liu et al., 2025b, Yang et al., 2025, Wang et al., 2025a, Liu et al., 2025a, Xia et al., 2025]. A parallel direction scales long-chain visual reasoning through structured supervision [Xu et al., 2025a, Thawakar et al., 2025]. These methods establish outcome-verifiable reinforcement learning as an effective post-training strategy for MLLMs, but final-answer rewards remain sparse with respect to whether the reasoning process is actually grounded in visual evidence. 

2.2 Faithful Multimodal Reasoning 

Faithful multimodal reasoning requires both faithful perception of task-relevant visual evidence and faithful use of that evidence in the reasoning chain. Prior work shows that MLLMs frequently hallucinate visual content or rely on insufficient visual evidence [Guan et al., 2024, Liu et al., 2023, 2024, Lu et al., 2024b], and that longer reasoning chains may amplify hallucination when not properly grounded [Xu et al., 2025b, Yao et al., 2025, Lu et al., 2025]. Recent RL methods introduce perception- and grounding-aware signals: Perception-R1 [Xiao et al., 2026] rewards consistency with visual annotations, Vision-SR1 [Li et al., 2026] decomposes responses into visual descriptions and language reasoning with self-rewarding, VPPO [Huang et al., 2026a] uses token-level visual dependency to reweight optimization toward visually pivotal tokens, and PAPO [Wang et al., 2025b] adds a perception-aware regularizer to the policy objective. A complementary line couples reasoning with explicit visual operations: Ground-R1 [Cao et al., 2025] rewards region selection, and DeepEyes [Zheng et al., 2025b] learns to “think with images” by interleaving visual operations into the chain. Our work addresses both halves jointly: we strengthen perception through supervision native to the image and explicitly target the perception–reasoning disconnect, a failure mode that cuts across existing efforts in which correctly perceived evidence is dropped or contradicted during reasoning. 

## 3 Proposed Approach 

3.1 Overview 

Figure 2 summarizes Faithful-MR1. The Anchoring stage (Section 3.2) augments standard supervised fine-tuning with a region-grounded attention loss on a dedicated <Focus> token, anchoring perception to task-relevant image regions. The Reinforcing stage (Section 3.3) initializes from the Anchoring-stage checkpoint and applies reinforcement learning: counterfactual image intervention localizes vision-dependent reasoning tokens, and a split GRPO advantage rewards answer-correct trajectories that concentrate visual attention at those tokens.

3.3 Reinforcing Visual Attention 

The Reinforcing stage targets the perception–reasoning disconnect by directly optimizing faithful use—a property that, as Section 1 noted, is not directly observable in the reasoning trace. We expose it through counterfactual image intervention: response tokens whose predictions shift when the visual evidence is masked are treated as where vision causally matters, and answer-correct trajectories that concentrate visual attention at those tokens are rewarded. The policy initializes from the Anchoring-stage checkpoint and inherits its <Focus> -anchored prompt format, so that “focus first” behavior carries over into the RL phase. 

Counterfactual localization of vision-dependent tokens. We construct a perturbed image by masking the annotated regions while keeping the rest of the image unchanged. Given a sampled response, the policy performs one forward pass on the original image and another on the perturbed image. Let pt and ˜pt denote the next-token distributions at response position t under the original and perturbed images. The visual sensitivity of token t is 

Dt = DKL (pt ∥ ˜pt). (7) Tokens with high Dt are causally vision-dependent: their predictions change when the critical visual evidence is removed. This turns evidence utilization from a trajectory-level notion into a token-level attribution problem—we explicitly localize the subset of response positions whose generation is causally supported by the image, in line with recent evidence that a small subset of critical tokens 

disproportionately drives reasoning quality [Lin et al., 2025].

## 4 Experiments 

We evaluate our method on both mathematical reasoning and general multimodal understanding benchmarks. We first describe the experimental setup, then present the main results. 54.1 Experimental Setup Training data, models, and baselines. Our training data is constructed from the open-source Vision-SR1-47K dataset [Li et al., 2026], with key visual regions annotated as bounding boxes; after filtering, we obtain 6K SFT examples and 13.2K RL examples (19.2K total). The bounding boxes supervise the <Focus> token in the Anchoring stage and define the masked regions for the Reinforcing stage’s counterfactual image intervention. We use Qwen2.5-VL-Instruct [Bai et al., 2025] as the backbone at the 3B and 7B scales. Under the same data setting we compare with GRPO [Shao et al., 2024] and VPPO [Huang et al., 2026a]; we additionally compare with recent public multimodal reasoning checkpoints Vision-R1-7B [Huang et al., 2026b], Perception-R1-7B [Xiao et al., 2026], and Vision-SR1-7B [Li et al., 2026], which are trained with substantially more data. 

Benchmarks and metrics. We evaluate on seven benchmarks covering math reasoning (MathVi-sion [Wang et al., 2024], MathVerse [Zhang et al., 2024], MathVista [Lu et al., 2024a], WeMath [Qiao et al., 2025], DynaMath [Zou et al., 2025]) and general visual question answering (MMMU-Pro [Yue et al., 2025], HallusionBench [Guan et al., 2024]). For DynaMath we report Worst Case Accuracy (WCA), the benchmark’s headline robustness metric where a question is counted correct only if the model is correct on all 10 visual variants. We report per-benchmark accuracy and average the available scores as the overall result. 

Implementation and evaluation. The Anchoring stage trains for one epoch with learning rate 

1 × 10 −5; the auxiliary <Focus> attention loss uses empirically chosen weight λ=0 .1 and target attention mass τ =0 .8 over the last 4 attention layers. The Reinforcing stage initializes from the Anchoring checkpoint and trains with 8 rollouts per prompt, rollout batch size 128, and learning rate 1 × 10 −6; the rule reward combines an answer-correctness reward and a format reward; the evidence-utilization reward uses threshold τg =2 and coefficient λattn =0 .3 at the 7B scale and 0.1 at the 3B scale. All evaluations use greedy decoding and are run with the EvalScope framework [Team, 2024]; for reliability, we use Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct [Team, 2025] as the LLM-as-a-judge across all benchmarks. 4.2 Main Results 

Table 1 reports results across seven multimodal reasoning and perception benchmarks, organized into two complementary comparisons: the lower blocks compare training methods under matched Qwen2.5-VL-Instruct backbones [Bai et al., 2025] and 19.2K training examples, while the upper block 6compares publicly released multimodal reasoning checkpoints trained at a range of data scales. Under matched backbone and data, Faithful-MR1 attains the best Overall score at both the 3B and 7B scales (43.9 and 51.3), and the lead extends across most individual benchmarks. Against publicly released checkpoints, Faithful-MR1 also surpasses Vision-SR1-7B and Vision-R1-7B on Overall and on most individual benchmarks despite using substantially less training data. This consistent advantage demonstrates the effectiveness of Faithful-MR1’s two-stage design, which anchors perception to task-relevant image regions and reinforces the faithful use of that evidence in the reasoning chain. Faithful-MR1’s lead is most pronounced on math benchmarks, where the reasoning chain depends most heavily on faithful use of visual evidence; on General VQA, where tasks lean more on broad textual descriptions of the image, Faithful-MR1 still attains the best average. The same pattern carries over to the 3B scale. DynaMath, in particular, offers the strictest test of whether reasoning actually relies on the image: under its Worst Case Accuracy protocol, a question is counted correct only if the model answers all ten visual variants of the same problem correctly, so reasoning chains that bypass the image are penalized directly. Faithful-MR1 leads here, consistent with improvements arising from how visual evidence is propagated through the reasoning chain rather than from polishing perception text alone.

4.4 Quantifying the Perception-Reasoning Disconnect 

To separate gains in faithful perception from gains in faithful use, we evaluate behavioral signatures on the MathVerse Vision-Only split (788 responses), which removes figure-related text from the question and thus forces models to rely on the image. For each problem we pre-extract a list of gold visual facts using Gemini-3-Flash conditioned on the im-age and the ground-truth answer; Gemini-3-Flash then scores every critical gold fact along two per-fact indicators: P =1 if the fact is verbalized cor-rectly, and U =1 if it is used as a load-bearing premise in the derivation with a value consistent with the gold and not contradicted later in the chain. We report Perception Coverage = Pr( P =1) , the rate at which gold facts are verbalized in the response; Faithful Use = Pr( U =1 | P =1) , the rate at which verbalized facts are then committed to the derivation; and Answer Acc. = Pr( A=1) . Perception Coverage and Faithful Use measure conceptually distinct quantities (a method can verbalize many visual facts decoratively, or commit fewer facts more faithfully to its derivation), so they need not move together. Table 3 shows that Perception Coverage and Accuracy do not move together. Vision-SR1-7B and Vision-R1-7B both attain high Perception Coverage but markedly lower Faithful Use; Vision-SR1 in particular leaves roughly half of its correctly verbalized facts unused or used with inconsistent values. Under matched backbone and training data, Faithful-MR1 reverses this profile, attaining the highest Faithful Use and Answer Accuracy most efficiently. The gain does not come from verbalizing more perception, but from committing a larger fraction of perceived evidence to the derivation, exactly the PRD reduction the Reinforcing stage targets. ## 5 Limitations 

While Faithful-MR1 delivers consistent gains across multimodal reasoning benchmarks on both the 3B and 7B variants of Qwen2.5-VL with substantially less training data, there are two limitations including its reliance on region-level annotations and the additional training-time compute introduced by counterfactual intervention. The Anchoring stage requires bounding-box annotations of question-relevant regions, which we obtain at scale from an automated multimodal annotator (Gemini-3-Flash). In addition, the Reinforcing stage’s counterfactual image intervention runs one extra masked-image forward pass per rollout during training, a modest overhead over vanilla GRPO; inference itself adds no overhead, and Faithful-MR1 produces shorter, more token-efficient responses than recent baselines (Table 3). 

## 6 Conclusion 

Multimodal RLVR yields smaller gains than its text-only counterpart: faithful multimodal reasoning requires both faithful perception and faithful use of task-relevant visual evidence, but current multi-modal RLVR methods address these only partially. Specifically, perception is typically supervised through generated text and faithful use is often overlooked, exposing the perception–reasoning discon-nect where correctly perceived evidence is dropped or contradicted during reasoning. Our quantitative analysis confirms PRD as a distinct failure mode beyond the reach of perception supervision alone, with final accuracy more directly influenced by faithful use than by faithful perception alone. To close these gaps, we proposed Faithful-MR1, a training framework that anchors and reinforces visual attention: the Anchoring stage supervises a <Focus> token’s attention directly against image regions rather than generated text, while the Reinforcing stage rewards answer-correct trajectories that concentrate visual attention where vision causally matters, exposed through counterfactual image intervention. ## A Additional Experimental Details 

A.1 Training and Evaluation Protocol Training data construction. We construct our training data from the open-source Vision-SR1-47K dataset [Li et al., 2026]. Key visual regions are annotated as bounding boxes by Gemini-3-Flash [Google DeepMind, 2026] conditioned on the image, query, and ground-truth answer (full prompt in Appendix A.3). For the SFT split, responses are generated by rollouts from Qwen2.5-VL-7B-Instruct [Bai et al., 2025] and filtered by rules and an LLM-as-a-judge for answer correctness and reasoning quality. After filtering we obtain 6K SFT examples and 13.2K RL examples (19.2K total). All matched-backbone baselines use this same split; GRPO uses only the final-answer reward, while VPPO and our method use the same answer reward with their respective faithfulness training signals. 

Implementation details. We implement the Anchoring stage with LlamaFactory [Zheng et al., 2024] and the Reinforcing stage with EasyR1 [Zheng et al., 2025a, Sheng et al., 2024], both with bf16 precision; rollout temperature is set to 1.0 in the Reinforcing stage. GRPO and VPPO baselines are reproduced from their official open-source implementations on our training data. All training runs are conducted on 32 AMD MI308X GPUs under the ROCm/HIP runtime.

(P1) Bounding-box region annotation (Gemini-3-Flash). Conditioned on the image, query, and ground-truth answer, we ask Gemini-3-Flash to localize the visual entities required to verify the answer as bounding boxes (normalized to [0 , 1000] ). 

Role : You are a Lead Visual Grounding Engineer specializing in Evidence-Based Localization. Your goal is to identify and ground only the essential visual anchors that constitute direct proof for the {QUERY} and {ANSWER} .

Task Objective : Analyze the {IMAGE} , {QUERY} , and {ANSWER} . Your mission is to provide precise bounding boxes for the essential visual entities strictly required to verify the correctness of the {ANSWER} . Do not label background clutter or entities that do not directly support the logical derivation from the {QUERY} to the {ANSWER} .
