Abstract 

Purpose : To investigate the value of preoperative enhanced computed tomography (CT) radiomics combined with clinical features in predicting the tumor response according to mRECIST of initial drug-eluting bead transarterial chemoembolization (DEB-TACE) in hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) patients by utilizing the Shapley additive explanations (SHAP) algorithm for model interpretation. 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS Methods : A retrospective analysis was conducted on 110 patients. Treatment response was evaluated using the modified Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors. Radiomic features were extracted from arterial phase computed tomography images and reduced using the least absolute shrinkage and selection operator. These features were combined with clinical indicators to construct predictive models, including logistic regression, naive Bayes, support vector machine, random forest, and XGBoost. The model performance was evaluated using the area under the curve, calibration curve, and decision curve. 

Results : The combined model showed optimal predictive performance, with area under the curve values of 0.838 and 0.802 for the training and test sets, respectively, statistically outperforming the single models (DeLong test, P< 0.05).Furthermore, SHAP analysis revealed key predictors and their directional effects. 

Conclusion : Preoperative enhanced computed tomography radiomics combined with clinical indicators can effectively predict the initial tumor response to DEB-TACE. SHAP visualization analysis enhances interpretability and identifies key predictors, offering support for precise treatment and individualized decision-making. 

Keywords : Hepatocellular carcinoma; Drug-eluting bead transarterial chemoembolization; Radiomics;Predictive model 

Introduction 

Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is a neoplasm characterized by high incidence 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS and malignancy rates worldwide[1]. The disease typically presents insidiously, with most patients receiving a diagnosis at the intermediate to advanced stages, at which point surgical resection is often precluded by disease progression[2]. For these patients, transarterial chemoembolization (TACE) is a critical therapeutic approach[3-5]. TACE inhibits tumour progression by administering chemotherapeutic agents directly into the arteries that supply the tumour in conjunction with embolic agents to obstruct blood flow. Drug-eluting bead transarterial chemoembolization (DEB-TACE), an advanced iteration of TACE, extends the duration of contact between the chemotherapeutic agents and the tumour while simultaneously minimizing systemic drug exposure. This modification reduces the occurrence of drug-related adverse events. This method has demonstrated superior tolerance and tumour necrosis effects compared with conventional TACE[6-8]. Nonetheless, the tumor response to DEB-TACE exhibits considerable individual variability; some patients experience minimal or no response, resulting not only in unnecessary side effects but also in the inefficient use of medical resources. For patients with hepatocellular carcinoma who do not respond to TACE treatment, timely switching to targeted therapy can significantly prolong overall survival (OS)[9-11].Consequently, accurate preoperative prediction of tumor response is essential for identifying patients who are likely to benefit. Radiomics achieves high-throughput quantitative analysis of medical images by integrating techniques such as digital image processing, machine learning, and 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS statistics, thereby enabling accurate personalized predictions[12-14]. Although some progress has been made in predicting the efficacy of conventional TACE[15-21], research on forecasting the efficacy of DEB-TACE remains in its early stages. Furthermore, many machine learning models currently lack intuitive interpretability. Our study utilizes SHAP analysis to investigate the prediction of DEB-TACE efficacy in HCC. We constructed a model combining CT radiomics and clinical indicators to improve both the transparency and clinical practicality of predicting treatment outcomes. 

Methods 

Study Subjects 

This study was approved by the institutional research committee of the Second Affiliated Hospital of Harbin Medical University. Because this was aretrospective study, the requirement for written informed consent from patients was waived. In this study, the clinical and imaging data of patients with HCC who underwent initial treatment with DEB-TACE at the Second Affiliated Hospital of Harbin Medical University between January 2021 and May 2025 were retrospectively collected. The inclusion criteria were as follows: (1) patients aged between 18 and 80 years; (2) a clinical or pathological confirmation of HCC; (3) DEB-TACE as the initial treatment modality; (4) an upper abdominal enhanced computed tomography (CT) scan conducted at our hospital within one month prior to the procedure, with image quality sufficient to meet diagnostic standards (absence 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS of severe artefacts and clear visualization of lesions and liver parenchyma); and (5) a follow-up CT scan performed 4 to 6 weeks post-procedure to assess treatment response. The exclusion criteria included (1) a history of liver surgery or liver transplantation; (2) the presence of other systemic malignancies or extensive systemic metastases; and (3) poor CT image quality due to severe artefacts or the inability to clearly delineate tumour boundaries ; and(4) main portal vein tumor thrombus or extensive PVTT (Cheng’s classification type III or IV), which is a strict contraindication for TACE due to the high risk of hepatic failure. 

CT image acquisition 

A GE Discovery 750 HD CT scanner was employed, utilizing a tube voltage of 120 kV and a tube current ranging from 200 to 250 mA. The slice thickness and interval were both set to 5 mm. Iohexol, at a concentration of 350 mg I/mL, served as the contrast agent and was administered at a rate of 3 mL/s. The scanning duration for the arterial phase was 25 to 30 seconds post-contrast injection, while portal venous phase scanning occurred 60 to 70 seconds later. 

Treatment Regimen and Response Evaluation 

> T

he Seldinger technique was used to access the right femoral artery to place a 5F sheath. A 5F catheter was then employed to select the superior mesenteric artery and celiac trunk for angiography to visualize tumour vascularization. Subsequently, a microcatheter was used to superselectively access the tumour-feeding artery and administer drug-eluting beads (50 mg of 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS pirarubicin). If residual tumour staining was observed, additional beads were sequentially injected until blood flow stasis was achieved. Before the end of the procedure, procedural success was strictly assessed using intraoperative digital subtraction angiography (DSA). We performed repeated angiography via the microcatheter to confirm the complete stasis of blood flow in the feeding arteries and the complete disappearance of tumour staining, thereby verifying the absence of residual tumour perfusion or arterialization.Treatment response was evaluated in accordance with the modified Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumors (mRECIST[22]). Treatment response was assessed via contrast-enhanced MRI/CT at 4 to 6 weeks post-treatment. Patients were subsequently categorized into a response group (CR/PR) and a nonresponse group (SD/PD) according to mRECIST criteria. 

Radiomics Feature Extraction and Segmentation 

3D Slicer software was used to manually delineate the three-dimensional volume of interest along the tumour margin, layer by layer, on arterial phase-enhanced CT images (Fig. 1). For patients with multiple lesions, the largest lesion was selected for feature extraction to represent the dominant tumour burden and align with mRECIST guidelines, thereby avoiding multi-lesion noise. Interobserver segmentation agreement was ensured using the intraclass correlation coefficient (ICC > 0.75), with discrepancies resolved by a senior physician. Radiomic features, including first-order, shape, and second-order texture features, were then extracted from the arterial phase tumors using 3D

Model Construction, Validation, and Clinical Value Assessment 

Patients were randomly divided into a training set (n=77) and a test set (n=33) at a 7:3 ratio. Given the limited total sample size, the training set was not internally split into a separate validation subset to avoid further reducing the training data. Instead, hyperparameters were tuned using cross-validation strictly within the training set, and a 10-fold out-of-fold (OOF) validation was additionally performed to assess internal generalizability. To prevent data leakage, all feature selection and dimensionality reduction—including Spearman correlation, LASSO regression for radiomics, and univariate analysis for clinical features—were strictly confined to the training set. Five machine learning 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS algorithms (LR, NB, SVM, RF, XGBoost) were used to build the radiomics models, while logistic regression was used for the clinical model. Model performance was evaluated on the independent test set primarily using the area under the ROC curve (AUC), alongside accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, F1 score, calibration curves, and decision curves. The DeLong test was utilized to statistically compare the AUCs among the different models. 

SHAP Explainability Analysis 

SHAP was utilized to analyse the interpretability of the optimal model across three levels: (1) Global interpretation: Summary and bar plots identified feature importance and contribution direction. (2) Local interpretation: Force plots explained the decision-making process for individual samples. (3) Feature dependence: Dependence plots illustrated how feature variations and interactions affected the model’s output.

From the initially extracted 1,715 radiomic features, dimensionality reduction identified six critical features and four significant clinical indicators. SHAP Explainability Analysis Results 

To elucidate the decision-making mechanism of the combined model, Shapley additive explanations (SHAP) analysis was performed (Fig. 5, Table 4). The SHAP summary and bar plots (Fig. 5) ranked logarithm-ngtdm-Busyness as the most critical predictor, followed by exponential-glcm-MCC and other features, quantifying their global importance. SHAP force plots revealed individual-level interactions, showing how features like exponential-glcm-MCC and platelet levels contributed to specific predictions. A key interaction was observed in the dependence plot: high exponential-glcm-MCC combined with elevated platelet levels synergistically intensified the negative impact on treatment response, confirming their combined detrimental effect. These findings enhance model interpretability by linking feature contributions to clinical outcomes.

Discussion 

We developed a combined CT radiomics-clinical model for predicting initial DEB-TACE tumor response, with promising performance over single-modality models (test set AUC = 0.802, P < 0.05). SHAP analysis visualized feature contributions, potentially enhancing clinical interpretability. Decision curve and 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS calibration analyses suggest potential clinical utility, supporting personalized treatment decisions. SHAP analysis showed radiomic features (e.g., tumor texture heterogeneity) may offer more granular predictive information than clinical indicators, highlighting radiomics’ potential to augment clinical assessment. This suggests radiomics could help clinicians optimize treatment strategies and improve patient outcomes in DEB-TACE management. SHAP analysis not only quantified the importance of features but also illuminated their potential clinical and biological significance. These findings indicate that the presence of a single tumour is a potential positive predictor of favourable treatment outcomes. These findings align with existing clinical knowledge, which suggests that patients with a single tumour generally exhibit higher complete response rates and lower recurrence rates following TACE[23, 24]. Conversely, elevated platelet levels are often associated with poor treatment response. As Peng et al.[25] have stated, a higher platelet count is a relevant factor for incomplete responses after TACE. This may be due to the promoting effect of platelets in the tumour microenvironment on tumour growth and metastasis. This carcinogenic mechanism was confirmed in the study by Jiang Lei [26], which indicated that the signals released by platelets induced the proliferation of breast cancer cells through the VEGF–integrin synergy pathway. An analysis of clinical features indicated that the absence of portal vein tumour thrombosis (PVTT) and liver cirrhosis are significant determinants of therapeutic response. As main trunk involvement was strictly excluded from our cohort, the presence 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS of PVTT in this study specifically represented segmental branch thrombi. Even so, the presence of PVTT has affected the efficacy of TACE[27]. This might be because PVTT causes an uneven distribution of embolic drugs in the liver, thereby reducing the therapeutic effect. Liver cirrhosis can affect the efficacy of TACE treatment[28, 29]. Additionally, liver cirrhosis may restrict the penetration and deposition of chemotherapy drugs due to the distortion of blood vessels and the decline in liver function reserve. SHAP analysis provided preliminary insights into the biological relevance of key imaging features. An intriguing paradox emerged regarding features reflecting intratumoral complexity: logarithm-ngtdm-Busyness and exponential-gldm-DependenceVarian ce exhibited opposing directional effects on DEB-TACE tumor response.Although both features indicate complexity, they operate at different spatial scales. High Busyness reflects a micro-scale, high-frequency fragmented texture, implying microscopic arteriovenous shunts that fail to trap drug-eluting beads. In contrast, high DependenceVariance reflects a macro-scale, patchy structural heterogeneity, which likely represents larger, irregular vascular compartments where embolic beads are mechanically trapped, leading to effective ischemia. Narang et al.[30] reported that abnormalities in the tumour vascular network could lead to uneven drug distribution, thus impacting overall efficacy. Similarly, Potiron et al.[31] confirmed that tumour vascular and parenchymal structures directly influence the penetration depth and retention time of drugs. 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS Nevertheless, we must acknowledge that given our relatively small sample size, this opposing effect could also be a coincidental finding specific to our cohort, and further validation in larger, independent datasets is warranted.The log-sigma-3-0-mm-3D-first-order-Kurtosis, as a first-order statistical feature, describes the sharpness of the CT value distribution. SHAP analysis suggested that a high kurtosis value is often associated with poor tumor response.. This may imply that the internal density distribution of the tumour is polarized, indicating the presence of large areas of homogeneous solid components or concentrated necrotic regions. This polarized internal environment may hinder the penetration of chemotherapeutic drugs or suggest that the tumour is in a drug-resistant pathological state. Grantab et al.[32] reported that the tight packing of tumour cells hinders drug penetration, whereas loosely arranged cells facilitate penetration. SHAP analysis revealed that exponential-glcm-MCC is an unfavourable predictor of treatment response. Its high value, which is represented on the left side of the distribution, predicts a lower probability of tumor response. This feature quantifies the texture complexity and heterogeneity within the tumour. The extreme disorder of such internal structures may hinder the uniform deposition of drug-eluting beads, thereby limiting the tumor response to DEB-TACE. As noted by Patkulkar et al.[33], high tumour heterogeneity is closely related to more aggressive disease progression and poorer prognosis. These radiomics features may reveal both the internal heterogeneity and the microenvironmental status of the tumour. Although the specific 

ACCEPTED MANUSCRIPT ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS ARTICLE IN PRESS pathophysiological mechanisms need further verification, they provide potential imaging evidence for a better clinical understanding of the differences in DEB-TACE tumor response. This study has several limitations. Most notably, we lacked an independent external validation cohort. Therefore, the model may only be applicable to similar patient populations, and its performance could be influenced by our specific imaging protocols and CT scanner type (GE Discovery 750 HD). Additionally, the retrospective, single-centre design with a relatively small sample size (n = 110) may introduce selection bias. Relying solely on arterial phase CT also limits the incorporation of multi-modal imaging information. 

Conclusion 

In summary, our combined model using CT radiomics and clinical features effectively predicts initial tumor response to DEB-TACE in HCC patients. SHAP analysis identified key predictors—such as single tumours, PVTT, and specific texture features—and clarified their influence on treatment response. These findings support precise, personalized decision-making for liver cancer patients.
